## Introduction

Social interactions represent the beginning, development, and culmination of ongoing attempts to ensure an NPC desired behavior or attitude towards a Player Character.

## PvP Social Actions

The Social mechanics for FxRPG are intended for Player Character (PC) towards Non-Player Character (NPC) interactions. They are not meant for enforcing behaviors between players.

However, players may choose to use the same mechanics among themselves in specific cases, such as when they are uncertain of a decision or wish to add dramatic tension trough a Test. When used this way, the results are not binding and depend entirely on player consent. Enforcing a result should only ever rely on mutual agreement and player honor.

Social rolls are not mind control, they never override player agency or dictate player character's thoughts or beliefs. Their purpose is to help a Referee tilt an NPC's attitude towards a logical or interesting resolution when that NPC would otherwise be undecided.

## Reaction Test

A Reaction Test represents the first impression an NPC has upon encountering the player characters.

- Make an Opposed Test of the PC's APP vs the NPC's FOC.

The outcome reflects the NPC's instinctive response to the character's Appearance.

## Impression Test

An Impression Test represents the final impression an NPC has upon finishing an interaction with the player characters.

- Make an Opposed Test of the PC's CHA vs the NPC's INS.

Although roleplaying should primarily determine the impression, the Referee may call for an Impression Roll whenever an NPC ultimate attitude towards the character is uncertain.

## Reaction and Impression Table

Use the following table to interpret the Degree of Effect. The positivity or negativity of the Effect affects the tone of the response. The Referee should interpret what this means for the scene and the participants.

| Degree | Adjective   | Description                                                                            |
| ------ | ----------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0      | Neutral     | Indifferent, like a routine interaction.                                               |
| 1      | Curious     | Notices something about the character and pays attention.                              |
| 2      | Wary        | Adjust tone or demeanor in response to the character.                                  |
| 3      | Partial     | Forms a mild preference or aversion; leans towards or away from character.             |
| 4      | Attuned     | Responds instinctively to the character; finds them appealing or repulsive             |
| 5      | Impressed   | Experiences a strong emotional or aesthetic reaction to the character.                 |
| 6      | Infatuated  | Develops a temporary fixation or rejection; emotionally engaged beyond reason.         |
| 7      | Moved       | Emotionally stirred by the interaction; the character leaves a deep impression.        |
| 8      | Assertive   | Express feelings openly, either trough words, gestures, or clear changes in attitude.  |
| 10     | Overwhelmed | Feelings intensify to admiration or hostility; self control begins to slip.            |
| 12     | Active      | Acts upon their emotions, takes initiative to support, oppose or pursue the character. |
