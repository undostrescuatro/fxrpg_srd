## Entering Action Mode

Action Mode begins whenever a situation demands precise, moment to moment decisions. It represents any situation where every second matters, most commonly during combat, but also in other time sensitive scenarios such as chases, traps or duels.

An Action is any deliberate effort made by a player that requires their character's full focus.

## Turn Time-Frames

Action Mode unfolds on a personal Time-Frame, this means second by second. Each participant receives one turn to perform a single action. Once every participant has acted, one second of in-game time has passed.

{: .note }
Other Time-Frames may involve different time spans for a single turn.

## Enabled and Disabled Participants

Participants in Action Mode fall into two categories:

- Enabled: Can act, even if they choose to do nothing.
- Disabled: Can't act due to being unconscious, restrained, asleep, or otherwise incapacitated.

Disabled participants are considered as having a Disabled Attack and Disabled Defense values.

{: .note }
This will be seen later in the Combat Interaction Rules.

## Turn Order and Initiative

Initiative is a static number, Higher values act first. In sudden situations, characters act in initiative order, no roll is required.

Generally, initiative follows commonsense:

- If a player declares an action first, they act first.
- Then they are followed by the others in initiative order.

{: .note }
Alternative methods of rolling initiative can be found on the Special Situations page.

## Action Types

During Action Mode, every choice a character makes is expressed as an Action. Each action is defined by four aspects:

| Aspects              | Details                                                                                      |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Enabled Defense**  | Your Evasion and Skills used to Deflect or Block Attacks                                     |
| **Enabled Attack**   | Any offensive Skill, such as Striking, Grappling, Throwing, and Weapon Skills                |
| **Alloted Movement** | The distance you can move as part of the action. Mounted characters use the mount's movement |
| **Adjustment**       | A small change in position, roughly 1 meter or a change in posture.                          |

You are considered to be performing your chosen action until your next turn. For example, if you made a Full Attack Action (which reduces defense), the penalty applies until you choose a new action.

If you have not acted yet on the first turn, you are considered to be taking the Doing Nothing Action.

## Free Actions

Free Actions can be performed at any time during another action. For example:

- **Talking**: You can speak briefly (about one second of dialogue). Ignore this limit if more speech adds to the drama.
- **Dropping an object**: You can drop anything you are holding at any point during your turn.
- **Using your senses**: Perception is constant and does not require an action.

## Doing Nothing Action

| Aspects               | Details        |
| --------------------- | -------------- |
| **Enabled Defense**   | Any as normal. |
| **Enabled Attack**    | None.          |
| **Allotted Movement** | None.          |
| **Adjustment**        | None.          |

Characters who are still, stunned or surprised are considered to be Doing Nothing.

## Take Time Action

| Aspects               | Details                                |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **Enabled Defense**   | Any as normal.                         |
| **Enabled Attack**    | None.                                  |
| **Allotted Movement** | None.                                  |
| **Adjustment**        | 2 Adjustment, if the action allows it. |

Used for non-challenging tasks that require several seconds or longer to complete.

Some Actions can be interrupted, others must be performed in succession until finished.

Examples for physical actions:

- Opening a door, retrieving, or storing a weapon, readying a shield, picking up an object, loading a firearm, mounting, or dismounting.

Examples of mental actions:

- Operating controls, concentrating on a spell, focusing your senses, or using a mental or social skill.

{: .note}
The Take Time Action reliably gives you 2 adjustments allowing characters to stand up from prone in a single turn.

## Move Action

Move up to your Jog Speed (or Combat speed for Machines). Taking Move actions in succession allows you to reach Running and Sprinting speeds.

| Aspects               | Details                                                                                          |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Enabled Defense**   | Any as normal.                                                                                   |
| **Enabled Attack**    | None.                                                                                            |
| **Allotted Movement** | up to your jog/combat speed. Then Running or Sprinting if taking a Move action on the next turn. |
| **Adjustment**        | 1 Adjustment.                                                                                    |

## Aim Action

| Aspects               | Details        |
| --------------------- | -------------- |
| **Enabled Defense**   | Any as normal. |
| **Enabled Attack**    | None.          |
| **Allotted Movement** | None.          |
| **Adjustment**        | 1 Adjustment.  |

Spend time focusing on a target to gain aim bonuses for your next Attack Action. Fumbles are ignored when Attacking after aiming, as this is kind of action implies heightened precision and focus.

- To make an Aim Action the target must be within Range and line of sight.
- Each Aim action adds +1D to your next Attack Action.

Close Combat & Thrown Weapons:

- You can add your future Attack Action's Allotted Movement to the weapon's range.
- Can only Aim up to a max of your Skill CPX.
- Evading or moving does not remove your Aim bonus.

Ranged Weapons:

- Can only Aim up to a max of the weapon's accuracy (ACC) rating.
- Ranged Weapons can be braced to gain a free +1D immediately in addition to the one gained by the aim action.
- Bracing requirements: for pistols (2 hands), for rifles (a flat surface or bipod).
- Evading or any forced movement removes your Aim bonus.

## Attack Action

| Aspects               | Details                      |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| **Enabled Defense**   | Any as normal.               |
| **Enabled Attack**    | Any as normal.               |
| **Allotted Movement** | Optional Jog/Combat distance |
| **Adjustment**        | 1 Adjustment.                |

Perform one attack using any Enabled Attack Skill. Weapons must be (set) to perform the attack.

- Use Close Combat rules for Melee and Unarmed Attacks Actions.
- Use Ranged Attack rules for firearms and projectile weapons.
- Apply any Aim bonuses gained from previous Aim Actions.

You may move up to your Jog distance as part of the attack, but you suffer penalties:

- You can Collide or Shove and no penalty.
- For Close Combat Attacks, a -1D penalty.
- For Ranged Combat Attacks, a -1D penalty or the weapon's BLK rating whichever is worse.

## Feint Action

| Aspects               | Details                   |
| --------------------- | ------------------------- |
| **Enabled Defense**   | Any as normal.            |
| **Enabled Attack**    | None, effect as described |
| **Allotted Movement** | None.                     |
| **Adjustment**        | 1 Adjustment.             |

A Feint misleads an opponent, lowering their defenses.

- Roll an Enabled Attack vs the Target's Enabled Defense or Creativity Attribute, whichever is better.
- On a Success, subtract the Degree of Success (DoS) as dice penalty from the Target's defense on your next turn.
- The penalties of the Defender only apply to Actions made by the one that successfully Feinted them and only last one turn.

## Full Attack Action

| Aspects               | Details                               |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| **Enabled Defense**   | Any, effect as described.             |
| **Enabled Attack**    | Any, effect as described.             |
| **Allotted Movement** | None, disables any optional movement. |
| **Adjustment**        | 1 Adjustment.                         |

Focus entirely on offense, sacrificing defense.

- -2D penalty to Active Defenses, until your next turn.

Close Combat Options:

- **Focused Attack** : +2D Aim bonus to an Attack Action.
- **Double Attack**: Two Attacks Actions.
- **Feint and Attack**: A Feint Action Followed by an Attack Action.

Ranged Combat Options:

- **Focused Shot**: +1D Aim bonus to an Attack Action.
- **Multifire** : See the Multifire rules.
- **Automatic Fire**: See the Automatic Fire rules.

## Full Defense Action

| Aspects               | Details                   |
| --------------------- | ------------------------- |
| **Enabled Defense**   | Any, effect as described. |
| **Enabled Attack**    | None.                     |
| **Allotted Movement** | None.                     |
| **Adjustment**        | 1 Adjustment.             |

Focus entirely on protecting yourself.

Options:

- Focused Defense: +2D to a single Enabled Defense.
- Double Defense: Make two separate Enabled Defenses against one attack.

## Disabled Action

Some actions are Physically or logically Disabled. They automatically fail, because they are impossible to be performed.

## Modifying Your Actions

### Preparing Actions

A Prepared Action allows you to set a trigger and react instantly when it occurs. If the trigger happens you interrupt that which triggers it. You must specify the action to be made after the trigger.

If the trigger does not happen, you can either:

- Stay prepared into the next round.
- End preparation and act normally on your next turn.

{: .example}
"I will **shoot** anyone who steps through that doorway"

### Waiting

Waiting is similar to preparing but you act after the trigger event rather than before it. Example "I will wait until anyone steps through that doorway".

- You do not specify an action in advance.
- You cannot interrupt the triggering action, only respond after it resolves.

{: .example}
"I will **wait** until anyone steps through that doorway"
