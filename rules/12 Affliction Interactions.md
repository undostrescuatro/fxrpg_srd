Sometimes internal or external elements impair how a character or machine functions. In FxRPG, these conditions are called Afflictions.

Afflictions represent gradual or persistent effects that deteriorate an entity's physical or mental state over time. They are resolved through Attribute Tests.

- **Rating**: The Threshold of the Test required to resist or recover from the Affliction.
- **Onset**: The time it takes for symptoms or effects to appear.
- **Effect**: The consequence of a failed test.
- **Interval**: The time that must pass before testing for the Affliction again.

Afflictions can affect any Attribute, each category represents a different type of strain or corruption on the character.

## Fitness Afflictions

Afflictions to Fitness (FIT) represent the limits of conditioning, These include natural strength, Applying forces, exhaustion, overexertion, and physical boundaries that a person experiences during strenuous situations.

## Constitution Afflictions

Afflictions to Constitution (CON) represent internal deterioration caused by starvation, toxin, diseases, or chemical agents.

In magical cases Some curses and spells Afflict a character's Constitution.

## Focus Afflictions

Afflictions to Focus (FOC) test a character's self control and mental conditioning. They cover addictions, compulsions, or impulses that a character struggles to resist. Focus Afflictions generally represent the mind's failure to maintain control over bodily or habitual urges.

## Instinct Afflictions

Afflictions to Instincts (INS) alter emotional stability and the primal side of human behavior. They include fear, rage, euphoria or magical compulsions that alter the mood and reactions.

Drugs enchantments and mental shocks often target Instinct.

## Addiction

Addiction is a Focus or Instinct Affliction that compels a character to seek a substance or behavior despite negative consequences. While primarily psychological, addiction can easily have physical effects over time.

Whenever an addicted character is presented with an opportunity to indulge:

- Make the relevant Test against the Addiction's Rating to resist.
- Success: You can safely avoid indulging.
- Failure: Allows refusal to indulge but they gain Stress equal to their Degree of Failure (DoF)
- Fumble: A Fumble counts as a DoF of 10.

If the source of the addiction is presented again after a period equal to the interval has passed, he must take another test.

Avoiding indulgence for a number of intervals equal to the Addiction's rating, The Addiction is considered partially cured. Lower the Addiction Rating by 1 and double the interval times. If the character manages the Addiction until the rating reaches 0, the dependency is cured.

Stress cause by the addiction can only be removed indulging in the addiction, otherwise this form of stress cannot be healed. And serves as the main reason a character relapses.

## Dependency Tests

Dependency is a Constitution or Fitness Affliction where a character must regularly consume or perform an action to maintain bodily function or avoid withdrawal and ailments.

Whenever a dependent character misses an interval.

- Make the relevant Test against the Affliction Rating.
- Success: Minor side effects, (no game effects).
- Failure: Add MoF to the character's Fatigue.
- Fumbles: counts as a MoF 10.

Wounds incurred by this type of Dependency can be lethal and cause extra effects depending on the type of wound.

On a Trauma Test caused by Fatigue:

- Light: Shakes fever and nausea.
- Heavy: Incapacitating shakes, fever, and nausea for the duration of a workday time-frame (2d6 hours).
- Critical: Deadly fever and convulsions for the duration of a full day time-frame (4d6 hours).
- Fumbles: Counts as a MoF of 10 and the character goes into Shock Trauma.

These wounds remain and only start their healing process if the character engages in the substance they are dependent on.

Avoiding intake for a number of intervals equal to the Dependency's rating, The Dependency is considered partially cured. Lower the Dependency Rating by 1 and double the interval times. If the character manages the Dependency until the rating reaches 0, the dependency is cured.

Certain dependencies representing medical conditions cannot be cured.

## Aging

Characters start aging when their age is above 40% of their lifespan. That means 40 years for humans (taking a 100-year lifespan) every 10% above said 40% (from 50% onwards) the Physical Health Tracker value is reduced by 1 to a minimum of 1.

## Example Afflictions

### Coma

- **Rating**: 5+AP (CON) or (FOC) caused by wounds or anxiety.
- **Onset**: Instantaneous.
- **Effect**: The character is Helpless.
- **Interval**: Monthly.

## Mechanical Afflictions

### Overheating

- **Rating**: 4.
- **Onset**: Instantaneous.
- **Effect**: The Machine Gains Light damage.
- **Interval**: Every Minute Time Combat speed held.

### Random Shutdown

- **Rating**: 3.
- **Onset**: Instantaneous.
- **Effect**: Machine must be restarted.
- **Interval**: every time it is used or every 10% of Deployment range.
