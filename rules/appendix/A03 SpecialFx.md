# Behavioral SpecialFx

Behavioral SpecialFx are a set of rules meant to alter the behavior of a character by providing them with a goal to accomplish in order to represent behaviors that are outside of the control of a character's rationale. Ex: fear of death, manias, OCD, and even strong convictions.

## Building Behavioral SpecialFx

### Decide the Difficulty Target Number

Define the specified behavior that the SpecialFx triggers. Ex:

- Kleptomania
- Compulsive liar
- Aversion to killing innocents
- Fear of Combat
- Celibacy

Select the Target number for the roll representing the difficulty of ignoring said behavior

Each difficulty has a base associated point cost

| Threshold | Base Price |
| --------- | ---------- |
| 1         | 4          |
| 2         | 9          |
| 3         | 16         |
| 4         | 25         |
| 5         | 36         |
| 6         | 49         |

Modify the cost of the SpecialFx depending on the possibility of occurrence according to the chronicle's theme.

- Check will be uncommon x0.5.
- Check is relevant x1
- Check can happen more often than normal x1.5

## Realistic Combat Behavior SpecialFx

FxRPG creates characters that are between reality and cinema. And it is considered that they are capable of experiencing adventure and the dangers that it entails. To represent the more average and regular human you may consider that PCs and NPCs start with the following SpecialFx.

### Combat Panic (Rating)

Mundane humans are not trained for combat. When bullets are whistling trough and people are getting stabbed, most people will look for cover against bullets or run the opposite distance of the attack's origin.

- Make a Threshold (Rating) FOC Test.
- Success: Character can act normally.
- Failure: The Degree of Failure is the number of turns in which the character must move away from danger or into cover and take the Do Nothing Action.
- Fumbles: Character is Frozen in place for MoF Turns.

### Aversion to Murder (Rating)

Mundane humans do not lean naturally towards murder. Even during fights most people try to get the other to back off or incapacitate them. And Even in self defense murder is still hard for a sane person.

- Whenever there is an action from a Character that would be considered lethal towards an innocent.
- Make a Threshold (Rating) Instinct 'INS' Test.
- Success: The Character can perform the lethal action.
- Failure: The Character cannot perform the action and can retry again after MoF Turns.
- Fumble: The Character cannot perform the action for the duration of the scene.

Consider limiting retries to 1 after failure. There is no point to the rule if the character can try until he succeeds.

### Traumatic Witnessing (Rating) of Suffering and Death

Mundane humans are affected by The stress of witnessing the suffering and death of other humans.

- Every Character that they kill increases their Minimum starting Stress by 1.
- Witnessing the suffering and death of others increases their stress by 1 per every 10 characters witnessed. Rounded Up.
- To lose this Stress you must purchase a Behavioral SpecialFx with a value equal to the amount of stress to be removed.
- The SpecialFx must be relevant to the circumstances that made the character gain the Stress.

### Adrenaline Rush (Rating)

Adrenaline rushes trough your body when taking damage. Delay the penalties of wounds by a number of seconds equal to the Rating.

### Unbalanced Handling

Swinging or Trusting the equipment makes the user loose their grip. They must spend the Rating in Take Time Actions to reaffirm their grip.
