## Positive Toughness SpecialFx

### Low Profile

The vehicle’s low silhouette makes it easier to conceal and harder to target. The rating cannot be higher than 2.

Grants the Rating to Obscurement, in Tough Terrain.

Cost= $Rating$

### Reinforced Systems

Sections of the vehicle are reinforced with additional armor plating, rugged mechanisms, or redundant subsystems. When the system is damaged, mark it off until repaired.

Number of times it ignores damage = Rating.

#### Reinforced Crew

The crew compartment is layered with additional armor and fitted with crash-absorbing material. The vehicle may ignore the first Crew hit on the Systems Damage Table.

Cost = $4*Rating$

#### Reinforced Fire Control

The Machine's Target computing systems are reinforced, or have backups.

Cost = $3*Rating$

#### Reinforced Structure

The frame of the vehicle is designed to absorb considerable punishment. The vehicle may ignore the first Structure hit on the Systems Damage Table.

Cost= $5*Rating$

#### Reinforced Movement

This protection is due to the inherent strength of the drive system’s design, or a built-in redundancy. The vehicle may ignore the first Movement hit on the Systems Damage Table.

Cost= $5*Rating$

### Haywire Reinforcement

Special insulation and grounding protect internal circuits from electrical disruption, reduce the damage level by one step (Critical → Heavy, Heavy → Light, Heavy → Unharmed).

Cost: $10$

## Negative Toughness SpecialFx

### Vulnerable to Haywire Effects

The machine’s systems are highly susceptible to electrical overloads.

Haywire weapons make three damage rolls instead of two. Double the Intensity of all electrical attacks against the vehicle.

Cost= $-7$

### Exposed Systems

Whenever Damage result occurs on Exposed System in the Systems Damage Table, increase the damage level by one step (Light → Heavy, Heavy → Critical).

#### Exposed Auxiliaries

External tools, equipment, or noncritical components lack armor protection.

Cost= $-5$

#### Exposed Crew

Crew members are partially or fully unprotected, as in open-top or convertible vehicles.

If only part of the crew is exposed reduce the cost accordingly. The machine has the Exposed crew SpecialFx until all exposed crew is dead.

Cost= $-10 (100\%)$; $-8 (80\%)$; $-6 (60\%)$; $-4 (40\%)$; $-2 (20\%)$

#### Exposed Movement

Motive systems (treads, rotors, wheels, etc.) are overly large or unarmored.

Cost= $-6$

#### Exposed Structure

The chassis is built from lighter, cheaper materials unsuited for heavy combat.

Cost= $-8$

#### Hazardous Ammo/Fuel

Fuel tanks or ammunition stores are poorly shielded or exposed.

Cost= $-3 (ammo)$, $Cost= -3 (fuel)$

## Positive Armor SpecialFx

### Ablative Armor

Special Armor plating that vaporizes or shatters on impact, dispersing incoming energy. Although it greatly improves protection the material must be replaced after battle.

- Each Facing must be purchased separately.
- Ablative Armor Deteriorates at double the rate.
- Maximum Ablative Armor Rating= TGH\*0.5.

Cost= $0.4*Rating$

### Heat-Resistant Armor

Armor panels designed to dissipate extreme heat or plasma energy from high-energy weapons such as lasers or shaped charges.

- Add the Armor Rating against HEAT based Energy Attacks.
- Each Facing must be purchased individually.
- Maximum Rating = 0.5\*TGH.

Cost= $0.25*Rating$

### Reinforced Armor

One or more defensive Sides are covered with heavier plating. One side for each Flank, front, and Back. Ideal for vehicles expected to face attacks from predictable directions.

Cost= $0.8*Rating$

## Negative Armor SpecialFx

### Heat Vulnerable

The armor and structure cannot withstand high energy attacks such as lasers or shaped-charge warheads. Subtract the rating of this Flaw from the Toughness rating of the vehicle when submitted to a HEAT attack.

Cost= $-1\*Rating$; Rating = Armor penalty to HEAT Attacks

### Weak Facing

The vehicle has a weak facing (arc of defense) because of incomplete armor coverage, shoddy design, or weak materials. When the vehicle is attacked on that side. Reduce Armor by the Rating.

Cost= $-1*(Armor Rating/4)$

## Positive Movement SpecialFx

### Diving Wings

Aerodynamic wing geometry allows smoother recovery from dives and stalls. Piloting Tests to recover from Stall and Dives grain a bonus equal to the Rating.

Cost= $3*Rating$

### Glider

Efficient aerodynamic design allowing controlled flight without continuous thrust.

- Looses only 1 Altitude Level or 1PM of speed per round while gliding.
- Can gain Altitude using thermal updrafts.

Cost= $2$

### Stratospheric Flight

Capable of operating beyond normal atmospheric limits. This allows transition from a flying movement type to a space movement type once the highest flying altitude is reached.

Cost= $5$

### Tuned for Turning

The movement type High Speed turning Threshold increases by 1 every 120° instead of every 60°.

Cost = $5$

## Negative Movement SpecialFx

### Cannot Glide

Lacks the wing surface or aerodynamic lift to sustain flight without thrust. If the engine fails it begins an uncontrolled fall.

Cost= $-5$

### Decreased Handling

Reduce the handling of during one movement types. Rating cannot make the handling go below 0.

Cost= $-3*Rating$

### Maximum Ceiling

Limited airframe strength or thrust prevents high-altitude operation. Subtract the rating in KM from 12 to find the maximum ceiling.

Cost= $-1.5*Rating$ ;

### Maximum Climbing Angle

Poor Vertical lift-to-thrust ratio limits climbing rate. The Aircraft must move The rating horizontally before climbing 1 altitude level.

Cost= $-3*Rating$

### No Engine

The machine has been designed without an engine or transmission system and must be towed, catapulted, or pushed. it Ignores the effects of hits on the Movement subsystem.

Cost= $-20$

### Poor off-Road Ability

Not designed for Poor or uneven terrain.

- Adds the rating to the Terrain Cost you can ignore the penalty by making a Piloting Test with a Threshold equal to 3 + Terrain MP.
- Failure: Light Failure to Movement Subsystem. Stuck for Half a Workday Timeframe 1d6 Hours.
- Fumble: Heavy Failure to Movement Subsystem. Stuck for a Full Workday Timeframe 2d6 Hours.

Cost= $-3$

### Poor Towing Capacity

The machine can't handle hauling, the vehicle’s towing capacity is halved.

Cost= $-1$

## General Machine SpecialFx

### Positive Auxiliary SpecialFx

These are systems that provide specialized bonuses or abilities to the vehicle, they are affected by the auxiliary systems damage result. If their action penalty drops to -5 or lower the system is destroyed.

### Acceleration Protection

Reduces inertial stress on the crew during high-speed maneuvers. Crew gains a +1 to all FIT and CON tests caused by acceleration, sharp turns or ramming forces.

Cost= $1$

### Airlift Winch

Heavy-Duty Hoisting system enables airborne extraction or delivery of people and cargo. The rating equals the number of people it can carry or 100kg of cargo per rating

Cost= $2*Rating$

### Autopilot

Simple onboard control logic for steady navigation and obstacle avoidance. Rating equals the Level of the Piloting Skill.

Cost= $EXP cost of Skill Level$

### Catapult

Launch Platform granting instant acceleration to aircraft or drones. it launches machines size equal or smaller to the Rating up to their Combat speed. If the launched vehicle has propulsion it may start at Top Speed.

Cost= $Rating$

### Emergency Medical

Built-in stabilization and trauma control systems.

- Cancels the Stunned crew results from Tests.
- Prevents loss of consciousness.
- Prevents wound deterioration for 24 hours.

Cost= $Rating$=# of Crew Actions Covered

Cost= $1*Rating$= # Number of Passengers

### Escape Seat

Ejection mechanisms that launch crew clear of a doomed machine.

Activates on Critical Damage that could kill the Crew. Ejecting one crew member safely with a parachute or beacon (space).

Cost= $Rating$= # of Crew

Cost= $0.1*Rating$= # of Passengers

### Escape Pod

Self-contained lifeboat capable of limited propulsion and atmospheric reentry. Its naval equivalent is the life raft.

Cost= $1.5*Rating$= # of Crew
Cost= $0.3*Rating$= # of Passengers

### Life Support (Limited)

Provides sealed atmosphere and minimal environmental support, some food, and waste disposal for each crew member up to the deployment range. If this system is destroyed while in a hostile environment, the entire crew is considered casualties, they are not all dead, just busy surviving to be able to fight.

Cost= $2*Rating=$ # of Crew Actions

Cost= $1*Rating$= # of Passengers

### Life Support (Full)

The full version of life support includes complete air recycling, proper waste disposal, hygienic and nutritional facilities and lasts infinitely for game purposes.

Cost= $4*Rating$= # of Crew Actions

Cost= $2*Rating$= # of Passengers

### Mining Equipment

Sampling, drilling, and earth-moving tools for terrain or asteroid work. Rating equals the number of work points it provides.

Cost= $Rating$

### Refueling Equipment

Allows mid operation fuel transfer between vehicles.

- Can refuel one target at a time.
- Refueling time = 1 minute per Size of target

$Cost = 4$; Boom

$Cost = 2$; Intake

### Gun Ports

Allows crew to fire small arms from within the machine.

- The firing ark is Fixed forward.
- Cannot Aim while moving.
- Can only fired small arms, not heavy weapons.

Cost= 2 for 1/3 of the crew

Cost= 4 for 1/2 of the crew

### Exit Doors

Large doors designed for rapid movement in and out of the machine.

Allows a number of passengers equal to the Rating to enter of exit the vehicle per turn.

Cost= $Rating$

### Loudspeakers

External broadcast system for communication or intimidation.

Cost= $1$

### Ram Plate

Reinforced Plating designed for collision tactics. Must be purchased once per facing. Reduced collision damage by half.

Cost = $6$

### Arms

Mechanical appendages for lifting, grabbing, or striking.

- Can lift objects of Size equal or lower than their rating.
- Rating cannot exceed the machine's Size.
- Several arms can work together to add up their rating but cannot lift more than double the machine's size.

Cost= $Rating$

### Battle Arm

The vehicle has a rudimentary arm, without a manipulator. Battle arms can lift heavy duty objects.Designed to punch opponents, they end in a reinforced battering ram or brawling weapon.

Cost= $0.5*Rating$

### Tool Arm

Utility Arm with built-in mechanical tool. Forgoes manipulation capabilities for specialized tool purposes.

Cost= $0.2*Rating$

## Structural SpecialFx

Structural SpecialFx are large, built-in features of a machine's hull or main body. They are not auxiliary systems and therefore do not take auxiliary system damage.

### Accommodations

Living quarters for long missions.

Cost= $Rating^\frac{1}{3}$ = Area Volume in Cubic Meters

### Air Drop Ready

Parachute, reinforced suspension and drivetrain for safe airdrop landing.

Cost= $6$

### Airlift Ready

Hard points and reinforcement for rapid VTOL pickup. Halve the times required for setting up airlifting.

Cost= $2$

### Cargo Bay

Hollow cargo volume inside the hull. The purpose must be specified to hold (Cargo, liquids, or Gases). One cost is for hull cargo and the other for Open cargo.

Cost= $Rating^\frac{1}{3}$ = Area Volume in Cubic Meters

Cost= $0.1*Rating^\frac{1}{3}$ = Area Volume in Cubic Meters

### Easy to Modify

Design allows rapid replacement and retrofitting of specific assemblies. SpecialFx must be purchased for every subsystem individually. Halve the regular work points required for repairs.

Cost= $3$

### High Towing Capacity

Heavy Duty drivetrain and frame for towing.

Multiplies the Towing capacity by the Rating, Adds the Rating to Shove Tests.

Cost= $5*Rating\%$

### Facilities

Onboard facilities dedicated to one Skill (except combat skills). For example: Medicine, Leadership, Engineering, Performance, Craft. Facilities are considered to have High grade tools and materials for work and repairs.

Rating = Number of professionals/tenants that can use the facilities.

Cost= $2*Rating$

### No Fuel Required

The main power plant draws ambient energy (solar, wind, ambient), deployment range simply represents maintenance times.

Cost= $10$ for internal systems

Cost= $5$ for external systems that can be cutoff.

### Reentry Systems

The Vehicle is fitted with the requirements to handle the stress of atmospheric reentry.

Cost= $2$; One use

Cost= $10$; permanent

### Stealth

Built with Stealth in mind, Gains the rating as a bonus when obscured. Also Active at night.

Cost = $5*Rating$

## Hostile Environment Protection SpecialFx

The machine is hardened against environmental extremes. Protection may include sealed hulls, airlocks thermal coatings, reinforced plating, and environmental interfaces. Life support itself is a separate SpecialFx.

- Dessert: resist sand ingress and abrasion. Cost = $2$
- Fire: reduce Intensity of fire by Rating. Cost = $Rating*2$
- Freezing: reduce Intensity of Freeze by Rating Cost = $Rating$
- Chemicals: reduce Intensity of Chemicals by Rating Cost = $Rating$
- Pressure: reduce Intensity of Pressure by Rating Cost = $Rating$, low pressure and high pressure separately.
- Radiation: reduce Intensity of Radiation by 20\*Rating Cost= $Rating$

## Negative Structural SpecialFx

### Difficult to Modify

Complex internals increase repair times.

Work Points required for repair multiplied by 1+(0.2\*Rating), SpecialFx applies to an individual subsystem.

Cost= $-1*Rating$

### Highly Flammable

Machine is built with flammable materials, catches, and sustains fire. Fire Damage last 1 turn longer per rating.

Cost= $-5*Rating$

### Inefficient Controls

One Subsystem requires an additional Take Time Action equal to the Rating to perform said action.

Cost= $-10*Rating$

### Large Sensor Profile

Machine is easy to detect by sensors. Gains a Rating penalty to Obscurement. This cannot bring obscurement lower than 0

Cost= $-4*Rating$

### Sensor Dependent

The cockpit is sealed and surroundings can only be seen with sensors and displays.If the environment permits, crew members may open a hatch and peak out, negating penalty but acquiring the Exposed Crew Flaw.

Cost= $-10$

### Traceable Emissions

Vehicle leaves a strong signature (heat, smoke, radio, tracks) Concealment is reduced by the current gear the machine has. Guided weapons automatically lock-on without need for a designator. The vehicle must be moving for this SpecialFx to take effect.

Cost= $-2\*Rating$

## Electromagnetic Engineering SpecialFx

### EW Suit

Electronic Warfare Suit, a device used to interfere with sensors and communications. Cost is related to the sensor's range. EAM and EDM are purchased separately.

Cost= $Sensor Range(km)+ CPX Rating$
