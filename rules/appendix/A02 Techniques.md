# Techniques

## Striking Techniques

The following techniques are unlocked by increasing the CPX of the Striking Skill.

### Elbow Strike

- CPX 1
- BD 0-0, Flank or Rear Facing Attack.

Ignore Facing Penalties or Grappling penalties.

### Knee Strike

- CPX 2
- BD 0-0, Front Facing Attack.

Usable while Grappling. Grants +1D to Targeted Attacks to the lower body.

### Flying Kick

- CPX 2

Use Striking to perform an Enabled Attack to Collide when entering Personal Space.

### Back Kick

- Flank or Rear Facing Attack.

Ignore penalties without changing Facing.

## Grappling Techniques

Once a grapple is established, you may use one of the following Grappling Techniques on subsequent turns.

### Takedown

- CPX 0
- Only on standing or crouching opponents.

Attempt to force your opponent to the ground.

- Success: Target is knocked down prone in your space or an adjacent one, Gain a Free followup Enabled Attack.
- Failure: The Grapple is broken, if the target is standing, they may attempt their own Takedown on their turn.
- Tie: The Grapple continues.
- Fumbles: if the Attacker has a Degree of Failure then the Attacker is knocked down prone instead.

### Pining

- CPX 0
- Only on prone targets.

Attempt to immobilize a target on the ground.

- Success: Target is pinned and cannot perform Actions.
- Failure: The Grapple is broken, if the target is standing, they may attempt their own Takedown on their turn.
- Tie: The Grapple continues.
- Fumbles: if the Attacker has a Degree of Failure then the Attacker is knocked down prone instead.

You can drag a pinned target.

### Joint Lock

- CPX 1
- Requires two free hands.

Perform Enabled Attack with your Grappling Skill. Damage is nonlethal.

### Joint Snap

- CPX 2
- Requires two free hands.

Perform an Enabled Attack with your Grappling Skill.

### Blood Choke

- CPX 3
- Requires two free hands.
- Requires targeting the neck.

A choke hold that cut off blood to the brain.

- Success: Target is treated as holding their breath during heavy activity. Reduce the remaining time by the MoS of the Attacker.
- Failure: The Grapple is broken.
- Tie: The Grapple continues.
- Fumbles: if the Attacker has a Degree of Failure then the Defender can make a free Takedown Attempt.

## Melee Weapon Techniques

### Iajutsu

- CPX 3
- Use an Adjustment to Ready a weapon that is sheathed.

### Fighting while sitting

- CPX 1,2
- Remove Penalties from attacking from a Static posture
