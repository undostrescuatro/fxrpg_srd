## General Terminology

## Chronicle

A chronicle is the game as it is being run and played. Do not think of the game as a game for making stories, but as a game that after is played, the played annecdotes become the story.

## Player

A player is a person who is participating in the game. Players create chronicles when they play the game and share the lived experiences together. Just like a normal gathering, a good player must show enthusiasm and engagement with the game and their fellow participants in order to keep an enjoyable mood during the game.

## Referee

The Referee runs the game, and is in charge of hosting the game. Just like a normal gathering, they must control the flow for everyone's enjoyment. This includes duties like: adjudicating the rules, managing player's attention, and keeping an enjoyable mood among the participants.

## Game Terminology

### Rating

A numeric value in the attribute of an entity. It represents how the attribute compares to their peers. In most cases rating is used as modifier for dice rolls.

- Ratings marked as zero are average.
- Ratings below average are marked with a negative number.
- Ratings above average are marked with a positive number.

This means that an average entity (human, car, spaceship) should have all ratings at 0 in all their attributes.

A rating different from zero is prefixed with a plus or minus symbol, for example '+1', '0', '-2'.

### SpecialFx

FxRPG is designed around humans and their limitations. By design, it focuses in representing the average population and not the uniqueness of each individual. To represent uniqueness FxRPG uses "SpecialFx", a set of rules that can be added into each entity, defining how it is able or unable to perform things when compared to the average. SpecialFx are also applicable to machines and equipment in order to differentiate one from another.

SpecialFx help represent things like:

- Nonhuman characteristics like flying, breathing water, or extra limbs.
- Human limitations like crippled limbs, disabilities, or other conditions.
- Machine quirks and extra functionality.

SpecialFx are designed with fun in mind and balance as a second priority. The Referee should keep this in mind when designing a chronicle. For example: A SpecialFx that causes a character to not need food or water may be very powerful in a survival chronicle but fairly useless in a chronicle in the middle of a metropolis.

It is recommended that the Referee picks and chooses which SpecialFx are available for the characters, or to adjust their prices accordingly.

### Tilde Notation

Numbers prefaced with the tilde ~ symbol are meant to represent approximate measures. When present on a table this means that the value extends to the approximated half point of the ones adjacent to it.

## Dice Terminology

### Dice

Dice are objects with marked sides that are thrown to generate random values. The FxRPG uses 6 sided dice to generate different kind of values.

For the purposes of the FxRPG rule-book, the singular form of Dice is Dice.

### Roll

A roll is the act of throwing dice and checking the resulting values. Dice rolls can be abbreviated in different ways through the book, by following these notations:

- $xD$ where $x$ is the number of 6 sided dice to be thrown.
- $xdy$ where $x$ is the number of dice to be thrown and $y$ is the number of faces in the dice. For example: a 6 sided dice would be $1d6$, two 10 sided dice would be $2d10$.

Dice marked with the Capital '$xD$' notation, follow the core test roll as defined in the basics section.

Dice marked with the $xdy$ notation follow a different set of rules, generally they are simply added together.

### Modifiers

The results of dice rolls are often influenced by modifiers, such as Attribute ratings or situational modifiers. Modifiers add or subtract to the total of a dice roll.

### Dice Bonus-Penalty

FxRPG has Dice penalties or Dice Bonuses, add or subtract dice from the total dice before rolling. If the total dice to be rolled is 0 or lower, you roll two dice and pick the lowest of the two.

Dice Bonus-Penalty is specified by the following notation ($+xD$ or $-xD$) where x is the number of dice to be added or subtracted.

### Fumble

A Fumble is a misfortune that can spell disaster. It is not caused by the incompetence of the character and is the result of environmental factors. Generally, the lower the Skill level, the likelier it will happen. Ex: The gun jams, the character floor gives away making him lose his balance, etc. The Referee determines what happens.

### Edge Dice

In fiction, characters gets a lucky break during daring moments. Edge dice are a pool of dice that are available to be used at any time on any test that involves the character. Edge dice do not prevent a fumble from happening but can help you succeed a test.

## Character Terminology

### Player Character

Player characters are those characters created with the intention of being controlled exclusively by the players during the chronicle.

### Non-Player Character

Non-Player characters are those characters created with the intention of being controlled by the referee during the chronicle.

### Points

Points are a form of Metadata. They don't describe capabilities and have very little relevance in the rules, but are useful for other purposes, like helping Referees and players gain information on the way something is built on the FxRPG rules.

Characters use these types of points:

- Attribute Points: Measure of a character's physical capabilities.
- Experience Points: Measure of a character's life and experiences.

Machines use these types of points:

- Machine Defensive Points: Measure of an object's defensive capabilities.
- Machine Offensive Points: Measure of an object's offensive capabilities.
- Machine Utility Points: Measure of an object's other non damage related uses.

Something with a high point value may be better than one with a lesser value, but it does not specify why. In a controlled scenario this could be true, but during a chronicle in a specific situation, something with a lesser value can come out on top. It is recommended to use points as a general guideline and not as some hard representation of capabilities.

#### Creativity Vs Knowledge

Creativity and Knowledge are two attributes that are often confused. Take this as a guide:

- Knowledge is Theoretical (Raw data disconnected from reality)
- Creativity is Practical (Applied to real world)

For Example:

- Knowledge is remembering the trigonometry formulas.
- Creativity is making the calculations.

A creative person may not know the chemical procedures that change raw food into a tasty meal, but he can certainly cook a delicious meal with any ingredients. A Knowledgeable person may know a recipe by memory but may fail if he has some missing ingredients.

### Skills

A skill is a learned technique or proficiency which can be improved with practical experience. The skills of people, creatures, and in some cases machines, are represented in terms of levels. Skill levels are represented with positive numbers from '0' to the infinite.

| Skill Lvl | Adjective | Description                                 |
| --------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| 0         | Untrained | No Skill                                    |
| 1         | Basic     | Minimal Skill, Hobby or introductory course |
| 2         | Qualified | Junior Skill, Work ready skills             |
| 3         | Veteran   | Senior Skill, with practiced experience     |
| 4         | Elite     | Seasoned Skill, exceptional among peers     |
| 5+        | Legendary | Fantasy and Superheroes                     |

Skill levels above 4 are considered fantastic and create a supernatural feel for the chronicle. Referees may consider keeping a maximum skill level of 4 or limit the number of skills that can reach level 5 to keep the chronicle with a more 'realistic' feel.

#### Skill Groups

Skill Groups identify and group skills that are closely related, its main purpose is to help contract the selection of available skills should a Referee wish to do so.

#### Skill Complexity

Some skills require more than practice. They need deeper professional study or technical understanding. This is shown by a skill's Complexity (CPX) level. While Skill level shows proficiency, Complexity shows understanding. For example:

- Sword Lvl 5 CPX 0 represents a master fighter with little theory.
- Sword Lvl 2 CPX 2 represents an adept skill with grasp of techniques and balance.

Considerations:

- All skills start at CPX 0, when first learned.
- If no CPX is listed on a test, assume the value is 0 for common knowledge.
- Complexity is personal, it is applied when interacting with Tasks, Objects, or Maneuvers that require a certain level of understanding, but are not compared to another character.

| CPX Lvl | Adjective | Description                            |
| ------- | --------- | -------------------------------------- |
| 0       | Common    | Self Taught, Obvious knowledge         |
| 1       | Beginner  | Basic, High School Training            |
| 2       | Technical | Trained, Vocational or College         |
| 3       | Advanced  | Bachelor, University Degree            |
| 4       | Expert    | Master, PhD Degree                     |
| 5       | Legendary | Fantastic, Creator of new technologies |

## Machine Terminology

FxRPG has rules for creating Machines, vehicles, and weapons used by the characters over the course of the game. Machines have the following attributes.

### Machine Attributes

| Attribute        | Acronym | Description                                                |
| ---------------- | ------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Size Rating      | SR      | General Dimensions                                         |
| Toughness        | TGH     | Structural Threshold                                       |
| Crew             | CRW     | Capacity for the piloting crew and passengers              |
| Fire Control     | FC      | Capacity to be aimed                                       |
| Sensors          | SEN     | Capacity to detect the environment                         |
| Communications   | COM     | Capacity to communicate and impede communication.          |
| Movement         | MVT     | Capacity to move (1 for each movement)                     |
