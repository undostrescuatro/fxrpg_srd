## Integrity & Damage Thresholds

Mental Integrity is defined by the Character's Determination Attribute. For Mental Damage the integrity is affected by Anxieties.

Anxiety are divided into three Thresholds:

| Anxiety  | Threshold | Description                     |
| -------- | --------- | ------------------------------- |
| Light    | 0.5\*DET  | Stressful, but not distracting. |
| Heavy    | DET       | Immediate behavior-changing.    |
| Critical | 2\*DET    | Permanent, insanity inducing.   |

## Damage Effects

An Anxiety applies an Action penalty (AP) and an effect:

| Anxiety  | AP  | Description                      |
| -------- | --- | -------------------------------- |
| Light    | -1  | Self Doubt, Anxiety.             |
| Heavy    | -2  | Isolation, Frustration, Rambling |
| Critical | -3  | Mental Breakdown, go insane.     |

- Effect: Characters must take a number of Take time Actions equal to the Action Penalty in order to be able to use mental skills each time it is used.

### Trigger Test

Any Anxiety gained forces a Trigger Test.

- Threshold 0 Focus Attribute Test.
- Modified by Action Penalty (AP).

| Wound Type | Failure | Success |
| ---------- | ------- | ------- |
| Light      | Stage 1 | Nothing |
| Heavy      | Stage 2 | Stage 1 |
| Critical   | Insane  | Stage 2 |

Stage 1 and Stage 2 refer to the fight, flight, or play dead response.

#### Acute Stress Response (Fight, Flight, or Play Dead)

Humans instinctively respond to stress in one the three ways: aggression, avoidance, or disassociation. Each character has one primary response type that represents their natural coping instinct. It usually remains constant unless heavy roleplaying justifies a change.

Mental Breakdowns are presented in stages and the effects vary depending on the response.

Stage 1 causes the target to act irrational for a minute time frame (1d6\*10 seconds).

- A fight response will threaten the source with violence.
- A Flight response will walk away and avoid line of sight.
- A disassociated response will remain in place questioning its validity in denial.

Stage 2 causes the target to act irrational for a quarter-hour frame (2d6+3 minutes).

- A fight response will attack the source.
- A Flight response will move away at maximum speed.
- A disassociated response will remain in place rambling about a possible explanation as to what is happening, will not attack unless provoked, and will not run unless he is injured.

## Mental Action Penalties

Action Penalties Stack, and apply only to rolls related to skills related to the mind, personality or perception.

Tracking Mental Damage Action Penalties is done using the Mental Health Tracker (MHT). Anxieties should be tracked individually as they heal or worsen independent of each other.

## Damage Stabilization

To stabilize one Anxiety a character simply needs rest from the situation that caused it.

| Anxiety  | Time             |
| -------- | ---------------- |
| Light    | $(1D-FOC)*1min$  |
| Heavy    | $(1D-FOC)*10min$ |
| Critical | $(1D-FOC)*60min$ |

A character can also be stabilized through empathy or therapy. To stabilize one Anxiety a doctor must pass a Threshold 0 Psychology Skill. Modified by the following:

- Patient's Focus Attribute (FOC).
- Patient's Action Penalty (AP).

Result:

- Success: Stabilize the worst Anxiety first.
- Failure: A quarter-hour time-frame passes (2d6+3 minutes).
- Fumble: the doctor gains a Light Anxiety.

Consideration:

- A person can't empathize with itself and must let the mental anxiety stabilize naturally.
- Some drugs like antidepressant and calming medicines can help stabilize Mental Anxiety.
- Referee may consider using other {lore} Skills or {Science} skills to try and help stabilize a patient.

## Damage Destabilization

Stabilized anxiety can be destabilized. Whenever a new form of mental damage is taken it destabilizes anxieties that are below their grade. This means Light anxieties never destabilize each other, heavy anxieties destabilize previous Light anxieties, and Critical anxieties destabilize previous heavy and Light anxieties.

## Damage Deterioration

Anxieties do not deteriorate.

## Damage Recovery

Recovery speeds depends on care level. All Anxieties recover at the same time. Anxieties Stay stabilized as long as the character does not trigger them again.

### Without Medical Aid

When Anxieties are left to recover by themselves, the mind stores them as traumas in the form of Insanity Points.

- Light: 1 year, gains 1 Insanity Point.
- Heavy: 6 Months, gains 4 Insanity Points.
- Critical: 1 Month, gains 9 Insanity points.

A character must spend their Insanity Points gaining a relevant SpecialFx to represent lasting trauma. If a player does not spend said points their Stress (xDR) starts at the value of Insanity Points they have accumulated.

Untreated Anxiety may worsen existing SpecialFx instead of creating a new one. Cultural views on mental health may influence how this manifest in roleplaying.

### With Medical Aid

- Halve the Without Medical Aid recovery times.

## Integrity Collapse

Mental Integrity Collapse occurs when A character is assailed by too many Anxieties causing them to alter their personalities and loose control of their rationality.

### Mental Breakdown

A character enters Mental Breakdown when their Mental Health Tracker (MHT) value is reduced to 0 by Action Penalties (AP), Mental Breakdown initiates a countdown to permanent insanity.

- Time to Permanent insanity: $(2*MHT-AP)*60Seconds$

Characters with doctor skills recognize a mental breakdown for free, others must make a Threshold 4 Untrained Psychology (KNO) Test to recognize Mental Breakdown and act accordingly.

### Reality Check

Reality Check is a special stabilization test.

- Success: As per stabilization rules.
- Failure: As per stabilization rules, reduce the time to insanity by Degree of Failure.
- Fumble: The Patient becomes permanently Insane.

If a successful Reality check do not reduce the AP so that the MHT goes above 0, Additional Reality checks are required. Recalculate the time to Permanent insanity accordingly. Continue until the character is sane or permanently insane.

## Mental Destruction

Mental Destruction is represented by Permanent Insanity, it occurs by:

- Failing a Trigger Test when gaining a Critical Anxiety.
- Reaching the Time to Permanent Insanity during a Mental Breakdown.

Expressions of insanity follow this pattern:

- A fight response will attack the source like a berserk until the source dies or the character is killed.
- A Flight response will move away at maximum speed from the current location until it reaches a place they would define as "home".
- A disassociated response will remain in place rambling unable to act coherently it has an Impossible Attack and an Impossible Defense and must be led around by other players.

in all cases the control over the character is lost. Unless a chronicle has a way to bring characters back from insanity. For Example: Mind backups, Magic, etc.

## Mental Armor

There is no inherent armor against Mental Damage. However, the Referee may introduce optional Mental Armor rules for specific chronicles:

- Certain {Lore} or {Science} Skills can add their Lvl and CPX Lvl as Mental Armor.
- Certain SpecialFx can add to Mental Armor.
- Spells in magical settings can add to mental armor.

### Mental Armor Deterioration

Mental Armor deteriorates as per the Universal rules.
Mental Armor repairs itself at 1 Armor per hour of rest, or 2 per hour of sleep.

## Mental Damage Special Rules

### Stress

Stress is tracked as a Damage Rating (xDR) value, separate from wounds, it begins at 0xDR and increases when a character endures prolonged mental exertion.

- After Combat with a horror, you gain Stress equal to the number of turns taken.
- Other situations can also make a character gain stress.
- Stress xDR naturally falls by 1 per hour of rest or 2 per hour of sleep.

After facing a horrible situation a Stress Test is made.

- Stress makes an Enabled Attack with a number of dice equal to the intensity of the situation, and xDR equal to the Tracked Stress.
- The character Enabled Defense uses the Focus Attribute.
- Stress wounds are stable and do not deteriorate.
