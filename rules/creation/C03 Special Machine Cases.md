# Special Machine Cases

The machine design system aims to allow the creation of any type of vehicle or object, from a gun to a spaceship, but this design focus comes with a limitation, and that is those machines that work as special cases. Either because they are too large or too small, or were created with a unique purpose, this section aims to cover those unusual cases and provide guidelines on how to handle them.

## Structural Machines

Large machines can be built as one large structure, broken down into smaller parts to facilitate construction, reduce costs and increase their survivability. First build a large machine that will work as the main hull and host the primary movement types. Then smaller component machines will be carried by the hull to perform more specific functions. For Example: Turrets, Comm towers, and Special Boosters.

Component machines are considered to be fixed in place. This has no bearing on the type of turret type that a machine equipment can have. The Deployment Range shows how many hours of power is stored in their internal reserves.

### Size

A structural machine can hold 10 components of a size 1 rating lower than the machine, said components slot can be further broken down if the machine sizes are smaller. The final fractional size is equal to the number of components it has.

Take into consideration that the Main hull has to tow the component machines and for that reason it has its towing capacity reduced by the mass of the component machines.

### Crew

Each section requires at least one crew member, human, or computer. Actions are determined separately for each part of the machine, including the main hull. Crew casualties are likewise applied to each separate section as damage is received.

Crew may be reassigned to other sections. A number of crew members equal to the Size of the smallest section involved in the exchange may be transferred each turn. Transferred crew members do not count for action purposes in the round during which they are transferring.

### Handling

Structural Machines are generally slower than small ones and their Handling rating should reflect this. All sections must have the same handling rating as the main hull. They use the same movement modifiers in combat. Sections ignore any handling-related damage result, but still lose Armor points as usual. They are affected by any Maneuver damage suffered by the main hull.

### Movement

Only the main hull machine needs one or more movement systems. Other sections do not need movement systems but still have connections to the main hull to benefit from its movement They may not take any movement related SpecialFx. Sections ignore any Movement-related damage result, but still lose Armor points as usual.

The exception to this is additional Component machine sections dedicated to locomotion. These contribute movement power by adding their total mass to define total towing capacity. Obviously, all component machines dedicated to movement must use the same movement type. If one of these components take critical damage or can no longer supply any power, the speed drops according to the regular towing rules.

### Toughness

Toughness is placed as normal on the main hull and components. Each section has its own toughness rating and takes damage separately. Should a section take critical damage, any remaining damage is applied to the toughness of the main hull to see if additional damage is suffered.

If the main hull Takes critical damage, the whole vehicle breaks up. Each section still has power for a number of turns equal to its Size, after which they lose all power and become inactive.

## Structural Machines as Buildings

Buildings and general structures such as roads and bridges are assigned Damage Capacities rather than a Toughness rating. The relative low toughness and large size of these structures makes it so light and heavy damage effects become irrelevant, and they take damage by slowly crumbling. When attacked the building is considered a stationary target and takes damage as normal. Instead of taking light or heavy damage the damage is reduced straight from the Damage capacity, when the capacity is reduced to zero the building is considered demolished.

## Combiner Machines

Combiner is simply a terminology to define machines that can work individually but can also combine with others of its kind to form a different machine using each combiner as a component.

The combiner and the combined form are designed separately, there is no need for them to have the same form, weapons, or utility systems, for example two walkers could combine into a flying vehicle.

Here are some considerations for the design of combiner machines:

- The weight of the combined machine should be equal to the total weight of the combiners that make part of its body.
- The combined machine’s crew must be accounted for by considering the combiners crews. Any crew the combined machine does not accommodate must be accommodated as a passenger or must be dropped off the machine before combining.
- Each combiner must be assigned to one or more of the following locations of the larger combined machine: crew, Structure, movement, Fire Control, and Auxiliary. Whenever one of these locations is hit, the individual combiner takes the armor damage depending on the damage result. If more than one machine is part of the location apply the damage randomly. If the combined machine gets a critical result only the combiner part is destroyed and the vehicle separates.
- The total General value is either the value of the combined machine or the sum of the combiners whichever is higher.

## Transformable Machines

Transformable machines can modify their shape and structure to accommodate for different functions and requirements.

The way transformable machines are made in the rules is by designing two machines that simply swap places in action, this is to allow maximum flexibility in the shapes available for use each with its strength and weakness.

Here are the considerations for the design of transformable machines:

- Each transformation is designed as a separate machine.
- All transformations should have the same build rating.
- Other ratings can be altered according to the design of the transformation.
- The cost of the machine is equal to the Highest general value machine plus 1/3rd of the other transformations.

## One Way Transformation

Some Transformable can only perform the transformation one way for example, jettisoning a booster pack or pieces of armor. A one way transformation means that it cannot transform back in the middle of combat and must be serviced by technicians in order to revert to its previous form. By the rules it works as a transformable, but the cost of the machine is equal to the general value of the primary form plus 1/4th of the preceding transformations.
