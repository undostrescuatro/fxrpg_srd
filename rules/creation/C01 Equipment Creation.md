# Equipment Creation

This section is used to create weapons and other specialized equipment used by the machines as integral parts of their construction or as attachments.

The equipment can also be used to create weapons and systems of any scale that don't need a “machine” to function. There are very little limits to what can be done, and it is just a matter of applying the right characteristics.

## Weapon Attributes

### Accuracy

This rating, abbreviated as (ACC), determines the maximum number of extra dice that the weapon grants when a character takes aim actions. When mounted on a machine, use the machine Fire control rating to determine the accuracy and equipment point value.

| Rating | Example                          | Cost Multiplier |
| ------ | -------------------------------- | --------------- |
| 0      | Nothing                          | 0.2             |
| 1      | Iron Sights                      | 0.4             |
| 2      | Recoil Dampening Mount           | 0.6             |
| 3      | Stabilization                    | 0.8             |
| 4      | Basic Targeting Radar            | 1               |
| 5      | Gyro Stabilization               | 2               |
| 6      | Improved Tracking Software       | 4               |
| 7      | Full Leading Reticle             | 6               |
| 8      | Self Correcting Weapon Equipment | 8               |
| 9      | Advanced Radar                   | 10              |
| 10     | Legendary Targeting Equipment    | 12              |

### Bulk Rating

This rating determines how comfortable is to handle the weapon while being carried by the hands of a character, a high rating represent uncomfortable aspects like weight, size, or shape, generally most equipment will follow the following table:

| BLK | Gear                                                                     |
| --- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 0   | Knives, Pistols, Grenades, Small thrown weapons                          |
| 1   | SMG, Hand Crossbows, Slings, Medium thrown weapons                       |
| 2   | Shotguns, Grenade Launchers, Crossbows, Small Bows, Large thrown weapons |
| 3   | Rifles, Machine Guns, Heavy Crossbows, Medium Bows, Nets                 |
| 4   | Sniper Rifles, Large Heavy weapons like bazookas and LAW weapons         |
| 5   | Cumbersome weapons that can be shot on the move                          |
| 6   | Indirect Fire weapons that need setup before shooting.                   |

Bulk Rating Also determines the number of setup actions that a character has to make in order to set up the weapon if it is stowed away. Weapons that are ready to be used in the open or stowed in special holsters designed for quick setup could require less setup actions.

### Complexity

Weapons have a complexity rating to represent how complex is the weapon to handle by using the complexity rules.

### Damage

Weapons have a Damage rating expressed as a multiplier and abbreviated as (xDR) this number is multiplied by the Degree of Success of an attack to determine the total damage done by the attack.

The damage multiplier also serves as a measure of the basic effectiveness of a shield or a flare launcher. In this case the higher damage multiplier usually means better armor.

### Base Distance

Weapons have a base distance rating which represents the range at which the weapons are useful in Hexes. This is because Machines can work in tactical scales different to the 1mt hex, for example spaceships and planes.

Close combat weapons express this rating as (x-y), ranged weapons express this rating as (BD) in the same way as the core rules do for personal scale combat.

Equipment can be used up to 5 times as far as its extreme range, but this is done with little chance of success. If used in space the difficulty of hitting at these distances prevents true line of sight range.

### Fire Arc

Design wise Equipment are considered to be mounted in static places in the machine, or in the hands of an operator. To allow targeting flexibility, an equipment can have a fire arc that represents the amount of horizontal movement that the equipment can perform when targeting. The fire arc alters the final cost of the equipment.

| Fire Arc            | Cost Multiplier |
| ------------------- | --------------- |
| Fixed Forward       | 0.5             |
| Forward 180°        | 1               |
| Turret 360°         | 2               |
| Slow Turret         | 1.5             |
| Handheld/Hard point | 1               |

Slow Turrets can turn around 360° but must choose a 180° fire arc with an Adjustment.

Handheld/Hard point means that the equipment requires a manipulator for it to acquire targets, this could be the hands of a character of manipulator arms of a machine. Without them this equipment can't acquire targets. Since the targeting is done by the operator.

### Weapon Type

#### Matter

This is used for most systems and for weapons that do damage by launching projectiles.

#### Missile

The Equipment is a rocket storage compartment containing one or more warheads. Missile systems are always single use but are linked for free to all other missiles of the same type. Any number can be launched at once at the same target as if its RoF was equal to the missiles launched. The Equipment can be defended against with antimissile fire.

#### Energy

This is used for most systems that work by applying energy (lasers, Gauss, shock, Plasma etc.)

#### Exotic

This is for weird devices such as psychic cannons, toxic chemicals, etc.

### Damage Type

#### Slashing

Weapons that can cause slashing damage halve the wound deterioration times of the wounds they cause, slashing damage can’t target small vitals like heart or eyes.

#### Piercing Damage

Weapons that can cause piercing damage can target small vitals like heart or eyes. Most ranged projectile weapons do piercing damage.

#### Blunt Damage

Weapons that can cause blunt damage ignore half of the armor rating of any armor that could be considered malleable (use common sense to determine this, ex: Kevlar). As a guideline, blunt weapons halve the armor rating of armor whose rating is lower than their Damage rating.

## Projectile Weapon Attributes

### Ammo Capacity

The Ammo capacity rating determines the amount of ammunition that the weapon can spend before needing to reload. The rules do not differentiate between the various types of ammunition, all that counts is the actual game effect, which is how many times the Equipment can be used. Use the table as a guideline. The Equipment will use the cost multiplier of the next higher entry. Example if a gun has 300 rounds, it would use the 500 line of the table. Theoretically the optimized choice would be to go for the weapon to have 500 rounds, but this table is to encourage a balanced view of the Subsystem’s usefulness.

| Ammo                    | Multiplier |
| ----------------------- | ---------- |
| 1                       | 0.1        |
| 2                       | 0.2        |
| 4                       | 0.4        |
| 8                       | 0.6        |
| 16                      | 0.8        |
| 32                      | 1          |

### Rate of Fire

This rating, abbreviated as (RoF) represents the number of projectiles or missiles the weapon can launch as part of an attack action.

Average Submachine guns have a rate of fire around 8 to 16 rounds per second. Rifles around 10 to 20 rounds per second. Machine guns around 10 to 30. And at the highest rates of fire a gun like the GAU-8 Avenger fire at around 60 rounds per second.

To get the rate of fire of a real firearm simply find their RPM (rounds per minute) and translate it to seconds. That is the FxRPG equivalent for the rate of fire.

Use this data as a general guideline when designing a weapon's rate of fire.

### Reload

The rating determines the number of setup actions needed in order to reload the weapon to its full ammo capacity. Some weapons can be reloaded cartridge by cartridge. Like revolvers and shotguns, these use an 'AxB’ notation in their reload rating meaning that an 'A' number of setup actions is needed to load a 'B' amount of cartridges.

## Combining Equipment

Equipment can be combined to buy several types of ammunition for a weapon, or multiple modes of firing, becoming a single piece of equipment instead of individual ones. It requires one Take Time Action to switch modes or ammunition feeds and only one mode can be used at a time. If the equipment is destroyed everything is destroyed.

Each Equipment adds and extra 5% of the sum of the systems linked per Equipment linked. Example: 2 10points systems would result in 2 extra (10+10)_0.1 for a 22 total, while ten 10 points linked systems would be 50 (10+10+10+10+10+10+10+10+10+10)_.5 for a 150 total.

## Linking Equipment

Equipment can be linked to shoot simultaneously, it can only shoot at the same target and in the same rate of fire. Linked equipment adds their RoF together. If a linked equipment is destroyed, reduce the RoF of the lost equipment while the rest keep functioning normally.

Systems linked to shoot simultaneously add an extra 10% of the sum of the systems linked per Equipment linked. Example: 2 10points systems would result in 4 extra (10+10)*0.2 for a 24 total, while ten 10 points linked systems would be 100 (10+10+10+10+10+10+10+10+10+10)*1 for a 200 total.

## Splitting Equipment

It is possible to split equipment into several smaller components. All the components have to be present for the equipment to work. But they don't have to be carried by the same user. The cost can be split in any way possible with the higher cost representing a respective proportion of the equipment. Then the split cost is added to the carrier’s value. For use all carriers have to be within close combat range and each one of them has to spend one action in order to use the equipment.

The components are damaged like normal equipment and any damage affects the whole Equipment using the largest penalty from any of the components. For example, if one carrier has a -2 from damage penalties and another has a -1, the combined Equipment would have a -2. If any of the components is destroyed then the equipment can't function.

## Equipment Point Value

Equipment has a value using the following formulas and rounded to the nearest whole number.

Ranged Equipment 'Base Cost': $(((DR^2+ROF)*0.1BD)-RR)*(Fire Arc Modifier)*(Ammo Modifier)$

The final point value depends depends on the final use of the equipment. Equipment mounted on machines uses the machine's fire control modifier, equipment used by characters uses the accuracy modifier.

Melee Equipment Base Distance Multiplier 'BDM': $(Maximum distance)-(Minimum Distance)+1$
Melee Equipment Base Cost: $(DR^2)*BDM$
