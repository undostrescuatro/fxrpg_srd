## Projectiles

Attacks that use projectiles fall into three categories:

- Fast Projectiles: bullets, mortars.
- Slow Projectiles: arrows, grenades, and Thrown weapons.
- Missiles: Guided or powered projectiles.

## Attacks

You can only attack with a weapon that is 'Ready'. All attacks are Opposed Tests against the target's Enabled or Disabled Defense.

An Enabled Attack is the use of a Skill or Attribute that would logically strike or disable an opponent, trough the use of the character's own body, or tools.

A Disabled Attack is an attack that cannot be made because the attacker has no way to act upon the target, there is no Test involved.

## Defenses

Enabled Defense involves one of three movements:

- Evade: Predict threats and move out of the way.
- Deflect: Redirect an Attack.
- Block: Interpose an object or body part against an Attack.

You can only attempt one type of Enabled Defense per Attack.

The use of an Enabled Defenses requires one the following:

- The character's Evasion Secondary Attribute (for evasion only).
- A Skill or Attribute that would logically negate an attack.

A Target has a Disabled Defense when it has no ability to react, such as being unconscious, unaware, bound or immobile.

- A Disabled Defense is a Test with a result of 1 (no fumbles).
- Apply modifiers related to environment, such as lighting or facing.

### Evading

Evading is moving your body out of harm's way. Fast projectiles, Evasion represents reading the attacker's intent rather than reacting after the shot.

- It can be used against any attack.
- You can evade multiple characters, each additional attacker in the same turn adds a -1D penalty.

### Blocking

Blocking means placing an object or a limb between you and the attack.

- You can only block Close Combat and Slow Projectile Attacks.
- You can Block once per turn.
- The blocking object must be 'Ready'.
- You must be able to curl (lift) the blocking object.

Results:

- Success: The attack is negated.
- Failure: The Blocking object's Armor Value is added to your own armor value against the Attack, armor damage is reduced from the blocking object first.

#### Blocking Unarmed

You may block unarmed using a free hand or limb with your Striking or Grappling Skill.

- Success: Forces the attacker to hit your limb instead of their intended target, The blocking target has a Disabled defense but add your DoS to the total, then compare it again against the original attack. Risky, but sometimes better than taking a hit to a vital part.
- Failure: lets the attack pass through.

### Deflecting

Deflecting redirects an attack using your weapon or free hand.

- You can only Deflect Close Combat Attacks.
- You can Deflect one attack per turn.
- Weapons must be 'Ready'.

#### Deflecting Unarmed

You can attempt to deflect with your bare hands or limbs using Striking or Grappling.

- Success: You deflect the attack and may initiate a free Grapple next turn.
- Failure: The attacker can target the limb you used to deflect with no penalty.

### Defending with Improvised Objects

You can Block or Deflect with any object that is 'Ready'. If the object resembles a known weapon, (e.g., a broom like a staff), use the matching Skill. If not, Roll unskilled.

## Enabled Defenses Special Options

You can perform these options as part of any Enabled Defense.

### Spotdodge

A Spotdodge is a quickstep to evade or reposition during close combat.

- use an Adjustment to move to your flank or rear.
- Gain +1 to Block, Deflect, or Evade against one Attack this turn.
- You remain within the attacker's reach for the duration of their attacks.
- If you can't move (no space or restrained), you can't Spotdodge.
- Crawling characters roll to the Flanks instead, and can't move to their Rear.

### Dropdown

A Dropdown is a dive towards the ground to avoid ranged fire.

- Use an Adjustment to move 1mt towards any direction on the ground.
- Gain +2 to Evade.
- If you land behind cover, apply the cover bonus to later attacks (not the first).
- You end your action prone.
- You can't drop if you are Prone or Crawling.

### Body-Guarding

You can take a hit for someone near you.

- The Ally must be within one Adjustment.
- The Attacker Test their Enabled Attack.
- The Body-Guard Test their Enabled Defense.

Success:

- The Body-Guard Becomes the target of the Attack.
- The Body-Guard has Disabled Defense but adds his DoS to his Defense total.
- Compare it against the attacker's original Attack again.

Considerations:

- Against Close Combat and Slow Projectiles, you can Block with shields as well as use Evasion.
- Against Fast Projectiles, you can only use Evasion.
- You can choose to do a Dropdown, as part of your Evasion, both Body-Guard and Guarded Target fall prone at the end of the Attack.

## Targeting Attacks

It is assumed that all attacks are directed towards the character’s center of mass. For a human this is the torso. However, A player can choose to specifically target another location.

For ease of play, measurements use the Size and Distance Rating Table from the Physical Interactions section, taking the average human Size Rating 5 as the baseline.

Modifiers apply not only to attacks aimed at specific body parts, but also to objects worn or carried that have a relative size to the human body.

### Target Size Modifiers

Targeted Attacks subtract dice from the attacker's Enabled Attack Test.

- For same sized targets, the modifier is always 0. (For humans, this is Size Rating 5)
- Subtract a number of dice equal to the difference in Size Rating.
- Ranged Combat Attacks have an additional -1D penalty.

{: .example }

> - Targeting a leg (Size 4) imposes a -1D penalty in Close Combat, and -2D in Ranged Combat.
> - Targeting an eye (Size 0) imposes a -5D penalty in Close Combat, and -6D in Ranged Combat.

{: .note}
is it recommended to use the Aim action before making a targeted attack, as the reduced dice pool increases the chances of a Fumble.

### Targets with Larger Size Rating

Most weapons in FxRPG are designed to be effective against targets of the same size of the wielder, for humans this is Size Rating 5. Weapons made to attack larger of smaller beings use the Target Size Rating (x) SpecialFx.

Attacks against targets larger than the attacker's Size Rating, grant a bonus equal to the size difference in dice.

## Targeting Vitals

Targeting small, vital areas (such as the heart, eyes or internal organs) require a Thrusting damage. These locations are often protected by surrounding tissue or bone, meaning other damage types would strike other parts of the body before reaching the intended vital area.

## Targeting Held Objects

When attacking an object held ex: a weapon, the attacker can choose between two intents:

- Disarm: On success, the target drops or looses the weapon.
- Destroy: On success, the object is the target of the attack.

Defenders cannot Block attempts to disarm or destroy their weapons if the weapon is the target of the attack, they can only Deflect or Evade. Unattended or inanimate objects are treated as having a Disabled Defense.

## To Hit

To affect an aware or wary target, the attacker and defender make an Opposed Test:

- The attacker Test their Enabled Attack.
- The defender Test their Enabled Defense.

Outcome:

- If the attacker wins: The Attack or the intended action succeeds.
- If the defender wins: The Attack or the intended action fails.
- If Tied: The Defender is unharmed. And something may happen.

As previously stated, Disabled Attacks and Defenses apply depending on the circumstances.

In a Tied result, the attack connects with the target (a glancing blow) but does no damage (multiplying by DoS 0). Contact may still cause something. For Example, a water attack making someone wet.

## Attacks in Personal or Restricted Spaces

When fighting or acting within a personal space or restricted area (e.g., a narrow corridor, crawlspace, or dense vegetation), wide swings and thrust are difficult to perform suffering a Dice Penalty equal to weapon's Bulk Rating (BLK).

## Close Combat Attacks

To perform Close Combat attacks, a character must have a weapon 'Ready', and the target must be within the weapon's reach. Some weapons can be used in multiple ways (by swinging, or thrusting them). The player must declare how the weapon is being used before Testing.

### Close Combat Attack Modifiers

Close Combat is fluid and full of movement, small positional adjustments rarely provide measurable bonuses or penalties. However, certain circumstances such as (terrain, facing, or posture) can affect Attack and Defense Tests at the Referee's discretion.

### Missing and Fumbles in Close Combat

Missing is of little consequence. Experienced fighters are used to recovering from failed swings and thrusts without loosing balance. Fumbling when Attacking or Defending causes the opponent to get a Free Attack (as they overextend or loose footing).

### Close Combat Stealth Attacks

Successful Stealth Attacks in Close Combat involve approaching the target undetected. it is an Opposed test.

- The Attacker Enabled Attack uses the lower of their Chosen Skill or Sneaking Skill.
- The Defender Enabled Defense uses the Sense (SEN) Attribute or Observation Skill.

{: .note}
This methods ensures the Attacker has the training both to hit and to sneak upon the enemy, as well as beating the natural senses of the Defender. Making Stealth Attacks hard to master, while keeping it to a Single Test.

### Close Combat Base Distance

Close Combat Attacks can only be made within weapon reach. Skilled combatants manage this range carefully, ensuring they remain inside their own reach while keeping opponents outside theirs. This reach is represented by the Weapon's Base Distance Rating.

Notation is (BD X-Y), where:

- X = Minimum effective distance.
- Y = Maximum effective distance.

A character can use an Adjustment as part of their action to adjust the reach of a weapon and keep the target within reach. For example, adjusting the grip on a lance.

Weapons with a Base Distance smaller than 1, are listed with either minimum or maximum distance of 0.

- Minimum BD 0-Y: The weapon can be used inside the target's Personal Space.
- Maximum BD 0-0: The weapon can only be used inside the target's Personal Space.

### Striking Unarmed

Striking functions as an Enabled Attack Skill performed with hands, feet, or limbs.

- The Enabled Attack uses the Striking Skill.
- The Base Distance is (BD 0-1).

If you Strike an armored area with an unarmed attack, you risk injuring yourself. If your attack succeeds, apply the following formula.

Damage = $ 'Target AR'\*MoS $

This damage is applied to the attacking limb, resolved normally.

### Grappling

Grappling functions as an Enabled Attack Skill that involves grabbing and restraining your target.

- The Enabled Attack uses the Grappling Skill.
- The Base Distance is (BD 0-1).
- The Attacker must have at least one free hand, or grabbing appendage.
- Attacking a prone, sitting, or kneeling target; Requires that your posture allows it, at the Referee's discretion.
- The Defender can use the Agility Attribute as an Enabled Defense.

After a successful Grapple:

- The Attacker does not do immediate damage.
- The Attacker ends its turn within the Defender's Personal Space.
- The Defender's actions towards the Attacker are penalized by the Attacker's Degree of Success (DoS).
- The Defender's Evasion level becomes 0 (untrained).
- The Defender cannot move away until they break free, or the Attacker releases them.

### Grappling Targeting

You may target specific body parts when grappling. In such cases:

- Use the Targeting rules.
- Only the chosen part is restrained.
- The affected limb suffers a penalty to Actions equal to the Attacker's Degree of Success (DoS).
- The effects of a successful grapple apply only to that body part.

{: .example }

> - Arm or Hand: Prevents use of the Limb or wielded weapon.
> - Leg: Trips or anchors the target.
> - Neck: Enables Choking.

To wrestle away an item, make an Opposed FIT test:

- Success: You take the item.
- Failure: You loose your grip.
- Tie: The grapple continues, with no winner.

### Actions During Grapple

An Attacker and Defender can still act within the limits of their restraint. They can:

- The Attacker can Release their Target, as a Free Action.
- The Defender can break free using an Enabled Defense.
- Use Enabled Attacks with a Base Range of 0.
- To use Aim or Take Time Actions the target must be pinned.

## Ranged Attacks

Ranged Attacks are Attacks made with weapons that shoot projectiles at a target, instead of direct contact.

### Distance Modifiers

The defining feature of any ranged weapon is their Base Distance Rating (BD), which defines its effective range. Weapons can attack beyond this range using distance increments, each of which doubles the previous distance and applies a -1 cumulative penalty to the attack roll. The relevant attribute of the attack changes with the distance. Attacks up to medium range use Agility, Attacks beyond that use creativity as prediction takes more importance over a stable hand.

| Distance    | Value | Modifier | Attribute |
| ----------- | ----- | -------- | --------- |
| Point Blank | $2m$  | +1       | AGI       |
| Short       | BD    | 0        | AGI       |
| Medium      | BD\*2 | -1       | AGI       |
| Long        | BD\*4 | -2       | CRE       |
| Extreme     | BD\*8 | -3       | CRE       |

### Displacement Modifiers

The target's current movement speed affects their ability to Evade incoming Ranged Attacks. The faster a target moves, the harder they are to hit.

| Displacement Speed | Modifier |
| ------------------ | -------- |
| Stationary         | 0        |
| Walking, Jogging   | +1       |
| Running            | +2       |
| Sprinting          | +3       |

### Missing in Ranged Combat

Ranged attacks involve projectiles that continue along their trajectory after a miss. When an attack misses, the projectile may strike another target in its path.

- Make a new Enabled Attack using 1D, this becomes a threshold for the new Targets.
- Adjust the Threshold with the distance modifier the weapon would have at the point of impact.
- Targets use their Enabled or Disabled defense. Displacement modifiers apply.
- The Attack uses the Random Hit Location rules.

### Fumbles in Ranged Combat

- If the Attacker Fumbles, The weapon he was using experiences a malfunction.
- If the Defender Fumbles, he slips and falls prone to the ground as he tries to evade the incoming projectile.

### Ranged Stealth Attacks

Successful Stealth Attacks in Ranged Combat are made against a target without revealing themselves to the Attacker. It is an Opposed test.

- The Attacker Enabled Attack uses the lower of their Chosen Skill or Camouflage Skill.
- The Defender Enabled Defense uses the Sense (SEN) Attribute or Observation Skill.
- If detection is logically impossible (due to range, concealment, or obscurement). The Defender has a Disabled Defense.

{: .note }
This methods ensures the Attacker has the training to shoot without being seen, as well as beating the natural senses of the Defender. Making Stealth Attacks hard to master, while keeping it to a Single Test.

### Throwing Weapons

Thrown weapons use the Throwing rules with these considerations:

- Do not calculate Base Distance, use the BD listed on the weapon.
- Do not calculate Damage Rating, use the xDR listed on the weapon.

### Indirect Fire

Indirect fire is exclusive to ground combat in gravity environments. (e.g., Mortars) they can arc over terrain or obstacles to hit targets beyond direct line of sight. Only equipment with the Indirect Fire SpecialFx can use this Attack.

An indirect fire Attack requires the following:

A forward Observer. An allied unit with Line of sight to the target.

- The forward Observer uses the Aim Action against the Target.
- The Aim Action uses the Electromagnetic Engineering Skill.
- A single forward Observer can relay info to multiple indirect fire Attackers.

An Indirect fire capable weapon.

- The Attack Aim Bonus is capped by the Forward Observer's current Aim bonus.
- Indirect fire can ignore an elevation equal to half the Target's distance.
- Indirect fire Misses behave like thrown weapon misses.

### Rate of Fire

Rate of Fire defines the number of projectiles a weapon can shoot in the span of a second. it allows the wielder to perform special kinds of Ranged Attacks.

### Non Automatic Weapons

Weapons with a Rate of Fire up to 6 are considered non automatic Weapons.

#### Multifire

Fire multiple projectiles per Action. The number of projectiles fired are limited:

- 1 + The Attacker's Enabled Attack CPX or the Weapon's RoF, Whichever is lower.
- Each shot is resolved as a separate Enabled Attack.

Multifire requires the Full Attack Action.

### Automatic Weapons

Weapons with a Rate of Fire (RoF) equal above 6 are considered automatic weapons. They can fire in burst or streams of projectiles within a single Action.

An automatic weapon can fire at any RoF up to its listed maximum, allowing the user to conserve ammunition. For example: a RoF 7 weapon may fire as RoF 6,5,4,3,2, or 1).

### Automatic Fire Options

Automatic Fire options require the Full Attack Action.

#### Burst Fire

The Attacker fires a tight, focused burst at a single target to increase damage.

- Spend a minimum of 6 RoF.
- Every 2 RoF spent adds +1 xDR (Damage rating).
- You spend RoF up to 6 times the Enabled Attack Skill CPX.

#### Walking Fire

The Attacker "walks" the weapon's fire across several targets or along a path.

- Declare the initial target of the attack
- Spend 6 RoF per target Attacked.
- Targets must be adjacent, otherwise count empty hex as an additional target.
- Test each Attack separately.
- You can walk fire a number of targets up to twice your Enabled Attack CPX.

#### Saturation Fire

Also known as suppression fire, this creates a danger zone that restricts movement or threatens anyone crossing it.

- Select a point within the weapon's medium range.
- Trace a line of hex wide per every 6 RoF spent.
- The line gains a number of extra meters for free equal to the Enabled Attack CPX.
- Test the Enabled Attack, this result becomes the Saturation Threshold.
- Draw a Triangle between the shooter and the line. This area is not Saturated.

Any creature or object within the are must:

- Evade vs the Saturation Threshold.
- Failure: the Creature or Object takes damage as if the Degree of Failure were the Attacker's Degree of Success.
- Use the Random Hit Location rules.

Characters inside the Saturation Area gain the "Combat Panic (Rating)" SpecialFx and must test against it. The rating is equal to 1 per every 6 RoF spent in the saturation fire.

#### Antimissile Fire

Antimissile represents defensive fire used to intercept and destroy incoming missile attacks.

- It can be used as an Enabled Defense against any incoming Missile Attack.
- It requires 6 RoF per Missile.

Equipment without the antimissile SpecialFx Suffer a -6 Penalty to Defend. 6 RoF can be spent to reduce the penalty by 1.

Resolution:

- Each Degree of Success reduces the incoming RoF by 1. Defend against the adjusted attack normally. If the RoF is reduced to 0 the missiles are destroyed.

## Electronic Warfare

Electronic Warfare (EW) cover all interactions related to communications, sensors, information sharing, target detection and remove weapon control. These interactions are governed by two opposing technologies:

- Electronic Attacks Measures (EAM): also known as Electronic countermeasures (ECM).
- Electronic Defense Measures (EDM): also known as Electronic counter-countermeasures (ECCM).

Both systems are used through machine and equipment.

### Requirements

- The equipment must have the Electronic Warfare Suite SpecialFx.
- A machine can have multiple pieces of equipment with the Electronic Warfare Suite SpecialFx.
- Each suite can perform only one EW Action per round.
- Activating and deactivating the EW suite requires A Take Time Action.
- Maintaining the same EW effect on subsequent turns requires no Actions.

### Electronic Attack Measures

EAM Actions are offensive uses of the electronic warfare suite designed to disrupt enemy operations.

#### Jam Communications

- Make a KNO Electromagnetic Engineering Skill Test.
- The result becomes a Threshold for any communication attempts within the EAM effective range.

#### Sensor Degradation

- Make a KNO Electromagnetic Engineering Skill Test.
- The result becomes an Obfuscation bonus for detection Attempts using Sensors.

Those Attempting Beat the EAM must:

- The EAM EW Suite's CPX is compared to the Sensor or Communications Device's CPX, the difference applies penalties to the person attempting to beat the EAM.
- Make an Electromagnetic Engineering Skill Test (CRE).
- Success: The attempt goes through.
- Failure: The attempt is blocked.
- Fumbles: Alerts the EAM EW of the attempt.

### Electronic Defense Measures

#### Counteract EAM

- Make a CRE Electromagnetic Engineering Skill Test.
- Target one Active EAM.
- If you beat the EAM Threshold, the EAM is ignored within your EW Suite's range for a number of turns equal to the MoS.

#### Missile Spoofing

- Make a CRE Electromagnetic Engineering Skill Test.
- Makes the Electromagnetic Engineering Skill an Enabled Defense against Missiles.

You can use a Prepared action to use this defense to defend others besides yourself.
