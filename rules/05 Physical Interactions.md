## Perception and Senses

A character's senses are constantly active and normally do not require tests. Only test when missing information would have serious or immediate consequences, such as detecting danger, spotting an ambush, or noticing something that may be overlooked.

### Understanding What Was Sensed

Sometimes perceiving something is not enough, the character must interpret what they sense. In such cases:

- Make a Personal Action Knowledge Test if the information could be recognized trough commonsense.
- Make a Personal Action Skill Test of the Appropriate Skill if it requires trained familiarity.

{: .example}

> - For Knowledge: A beeping sound might come from a car alarm, a timer, or a heart monitor.
> - For Skills:
>   - Engineering can be used to diagnose machine noises.
>   - Biology to identify animal sounds.
>   - Craft (Cooking) or (poison) to identify consumables.
>   - Craft (Sculpting) for touch.

## Vision

Vision is one of humanity's strongest senses, but it is limited by distance, lighting, and obstructions. Characters can see for kilometers under normal conditions, line of sight ends at around 5km due the curvature of the horizon, and visibility is restricted in darkness and brightness, or by the spectrum of light humans can see. (infrared and ultraviolet)

| Degree | Effect                                                             |
| -----: | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
|     -3 | The character was not able to see anything                         |
|     -2 | The character was not able to see anything                         |
|     -1 | The character was not able to see anything                         |
|      0 | The character saw movement, but did not gather any info            |
|      1 | The character saw movement, and the general direction              |
|      2 | The character saw movement, the general direction and general size |
|      3 | The character saw everything                                       |

## Hearing

Hearing often detect things before they are seen. Its limits depend on volume, distance, and frequency (ultrasonic and subsonic). Test only when the sound is masked by other sounds or barely audible at the distance involved.

| Degree | Effect                                                     |
| -----: | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
|     -3 | The character was not able to hear anything                |
|     -2 | The character was not able to hear anything                |
|     -1 | The character was not able to hear anything                |
|      0 | Heard, but knows not where, or what it heard.              |
|      1 | Heard, knows general direction and details                 |
|      2 | Heard, knows general direction and if the source is moving |
|      3 | Heard the sound clearly                                    |

## Taste, Smell, and Touch

These senses usually require direct contact and rarely call for a Test. If you can already taste, smell, or feel something you passed the test. The important part is identifying it, not perceiving it.

Tests are only required for very subtle sensations, such as:

- A hidden transmitter under the clothes.
- A faint prick of a needle.
- A barely detectable flavor or scent.

In these cases make a Personal Action Sense Test with the desired Threshold.

## Detecting and Concealment

Characters first detect a target before they can interact with it.

### Requirements for Detection

- The Target is within sensory range.
- There is no terrain blocking the sensing.
- The Detection is higher than the Target's Concealment.

### Obscurement

Obscurement represents how terrain or environmental conditions make a target harder to detect.

- The total Obscurement is the sum of terrain obscurement between the Detector and the Target.
- If elevation allows view over some of the terrain ignore it.
- The obscurement of the terrain where the target is, cannot be ignored.
- Obscurement adds CPX to detection Test.
- Some terrain have a CPX requirement to effectively hide in them.

### Detection and Concealment

- Detectors are always assumed to be passively detecting their surroundings, if a skill can't be used, they can default to a Sense Attribute Test if the circumstances allow it.
- There is no passive Concealing, a character must choose to become concealed and is concealed as long as it is logical to do so.
- Opposed Detection tests are triggered by actions that could reveal the concealed to the detector.
  - For revealing sounds: Test the Sneaking Skill.
  - For revealing visuals: Test the Camouflage Skill.
  - For hiding within a crowd in plain sight: Test the Shadowing Skill.

{: .note }
Sense Attribute test is a baseline defense against concealment. Making the Sneaking, Camouflage, Observation, and Search Skills Require more training before they become effective.

#### Machine Detection

Characters can be assisted by machines in order to detect concealed targets.

- Machine detection such as radars, lidars, and other mechanical methods use the Respective Engineering Skill.
- Some sensors grant different bonuses or increased capabilities depending on their rating and type. For example: Motion detectors, infrared optics, sonars.
- A Machine's sensor Rating Matches the CPX against the CPX of Detection Tests.

##### Increasing Sensor Range

A machine can increase its Sensor's range by increasing power. This has the following effect:

- The Base Distance of the Sensor is doubled.
- The Sensor gains +1 to their Rating.
- The Machine gains a -1 to Obscurement.

On a planetary surface the maximum sensor range is equal to the distance to the horizon. This distance can be increased in the following ways:

- Place the unit in a high point, increased height increases the distance towards the horizon.
- Using a sensor Array, or Sharing sensor information.

##### Sensors Array

Machines can deploy a towed sensor array behind them.

The array increases the sensor range by an amount equal to its length. The maximum length of a towed array is equal to the sensor's Base Range. The towed array is severed and lost if the vehicle moves at Top Speed, or make harsh turns.

##### Underwater Sensors

Underwater sensors can detect units up to their range squared, but everything outside of it is undetectable.

## Size and Distance Rating

The FxRPG system abstracts size and distance into simple categories for ease of play. Rather than tracking exact measurements, the game uses natural language ranges that feel intuitive during play. This sacrifices precision for clarity and speed.

Distance is measured from the Origin to the Target

| ------ | -------------- | ------------- | --------------------------------- | ----------- | ------------- |
| 0      | Personal space | Trench        | Eye, Coin, grenade                | ~$1m$       | ~$0.5kg$      |
| 1      | Close          | Ditch         | Neck, palm, pistol, SMG           | ~$5m$       | ~$1kg$        |
| 2      | Short          | Sinkhole      | Head, shotgun, Grenade Launcher   | ~$10m$      | ~$5kg$        |
| 3      | Medium         | Canyon        | Arm, rifle, Machine Gun, crossbow | ~$50m$      | ~$10kg$       |
| 4      | Long           | Chasm Rim     | Leg, long rifle                   | ~$100m$     | ~$50kg$       |
| 5      | Far            | Chasm Wall    | Humans, Tripod gun                | ~$500m$     | ~$100kg$      |

Size ratings are mostly relevant for:

- Vehicles, Buildings, and large creatures.
- Environmental or slam attacks, where mass and scale matters.
- Aiming at locations of equivalent size.

{: .note }

> Coin Eclipse Principle.
>
> When an object's Size Rating equals its Distance Rating, it appears roughly the same apparent size as any object of equal ratio. For example:
>
> - A person (size 5) viewed from 500m (distance 5)
> - A plane (size 8) viewed from 50km (distance 8)
> - A ship (size 9) viewed from 100km (distance 9)
>
> All would appear roughly coin-sized from that distance.

## Readying Objects for Use

An object is (Ready) when it is the user's hand and in a position that allows handling for effective use. If it is stored, slung, or otherwise out of hand it is not ready. readying or handling items usually require Take Time Actions. For example:

- Exchange an object: Both parties spend 1 Take Time Action, the receiver now has the item ready in their hands.
- Pick up an object: You must be in a posture that allows reaching the object. The item becomes ready in the hand.
- Readying Bulky items: Spend a number of Take Time Actions equal to the item's BLK Rating (minimum 1).
- Reload a weapon: Spend a number of Take Time Actions equal to the weapon's Reload Rating.

## Facing

Characters are assumed to face forward by default. Most actions automatically include turning towards their target. If a character cannot reorient but must act in another direction, apply a Facing Penalty based on the angle.

| Facing | Penalty | Example      |
| ------ | ------- | ------------ |
| Front  | 0       | Face to Face |
| Flanks | -1      | Sides        |
| Back   | -2      | Back         |

These modifiers apply to both offense and defense. For instance, being attacked from behind imposes a -2 to defense, a back kick likewise suffers a -2 to attack.

## Postures

Changing Posture generally requires spending Adjustment as part of an action. A character cannot go directly from prone to upright in a single Adjustment, they must first be kneeling, sitting, or crawling. Conversely, dropping form upright to prone takes only one Adjustment.

{: .note}

> When prone, your torso counts as a limb sized target for ranged attacks.
>
> - Front the front: Hits to legs or vitals count as torso hits.
> - Front the back: Hits to head or arms count as torso hits.

### Static Postures

Static postures do not allow movement beyond the ones allowed by adjustments.

| Posture  | Enabled Attack Modifier | Enabled Defense Modifier           |
| -------- | ----------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Sitting  | -1, Melee only.         | -1                                 |
| Kneeling | -1, Melee only.         | -1, +1 to Block Shove and Collide. |
| Prone    | -2, Melee only.         | 0, -2 vs Melee.                    |

### Dynamic Postures

Dynamic postures allow movement, but limit maximum speed.

| Posture   | Top Speed | Enabled Attack Modifier | Enabled Defense Modifier          |
| --------- | --------- | ----------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Upright   | Sprint    | 0                       | 0                                 |
| Crouching | Walk      | 0                       | 0, +1 to Block Shove and Collide. |
| Crawling  | Crawling  | -2, Melee only.         | 0, -1 vs Melee.                   |

## Ground Movement

Characters can move freely without requiring a physical game board. As long as the Referee and players share a clear mental picture of the environment and relative positions, the game should flow smoothly. If a map is used, FxRPG recommends hexes for tactical play.

### Speed

There are six movement speeds. These ratings represent the maximum displacement per turn. Because different environments may vary in distance (e.g., planes, spaceships), these values are also abstracted into Move Points.

| Vehicle      | Gear | Walker     | Move Points             |
| ------------ | ---- | ---------- | ----------------------- |
| Top Speed    | 5th  | Sprinting  | $Top Speed$             |
| High Speed   | 4th  | Running    | $\frac{2}{3}*Top Speed$ |
| Combat Speed | 3rd  | Jogging    | $\frac{1}{2}*Top Speed$ |
| Reverse      | 2nd  | Walking    | $\frac{1}{3}*Top Speed$ |
| Starting     | 1st  | Crawling   | $\frac{1}{6}*Top Speed$ |
| Adjustment   | 1st  | Adjustment | $\frac{1}{6}*Top Speed$ |

Round all fractions to the nearest whole (minimum 1) for simplicity in play.

#### Defining Top Speed

A character's Top Speed Under normal conditions is the sprint, sprints starts at 6 meters plus 1 meter per point of Fitness (FIT).

Machines define their Top Speed as part of their construction rules.

#### Crawling

The character moves while lying prone. While slow it provides better cover against ranged attacks and makes the character a smaller target. Crawling can be the only option in tight spaces such as vents, caves, or tunnels.

#### Walking

The character moves at an unhurried pace, allowing for quick changes in direction and situational awareness. Walking is not tiring and includes crouch-walking, commonly used in combat to minimize exposure.

#### Jogging

A steady pace between walking and running, fast but still controlled. Jogging is the default Move Action speed in combat, balancing mobility and reactions.

#### Running

A fast pace that sacrifices precision. Running limits other actions, specially those requiring aim or dexterity. it is also fatiguing.

#### Sprinting

A burst of speed, the fastest movement possible. While sprinting, no other actions can be taken, and turning sharply is difficult.

#### Adjustment

An Adjustment represents a minor movement. Roughly 1 meter of movement, or a change in posture (see Posture Rules).

#### Reverse

Machines moving at Starting or Reverse speeds (1st and 2nd Gear) can choose to move backward or forward. Reverse movement is not possible at higher speeds.

#### Acceleration and Sprinting

Unlike organic movement, mechanical acceleration is gradual. A Vehicle must build up to its Top Speed by increasing its speed tier each turn. Instant acceleration to top speed (sprinting) is only possible for Walker movement Types.

#### Turning at High Speeds

Multiple turns can be performed throughout a movement. At speeds above Running and 4th gear, each turn must be followed by at least one hex of forward movement before another can be performed.

To Skip the required forward movement.

- Make a Test with a Threshold of 1 for every 60° turned.
- Characters Test the Agility Attribute or Running Skill.
- Machines Test the Piloting Skill.

## Personal Space

A Human sized character requires roughly 1 meter of personal space. This means two characters can stand side by side in a two meter corridor, effectively blocking passage to anyone trying to move past them. For simplicity, most doors are assumed to be 1 meter wide, allowing only one person to pass or hold the doorway at a time.

This 1 meter assumption represents the minimum space needed to perform active Attacks and Defenses. If the available space is smaller, like a cramped hallway or vent, the Referee may rule that these would be Disabled Actions.

## Invading the Personal Space

A character can move through an ally's space without issue. If an enemy chooses to allow passage, they also pass without issue. Opposing enemies, however, must be bypassed or confronted. If the only way forward is trough an enemy's personal space, the character must attempt one of the following actions:

- Avoid: Attempt to slip past without direct contact.
- Shove: Attempt to push the opponent aside.
- Collide: Charge through with brute force.

A character cannot invade personal space if:

- They are currently grappled or otherwise restrained.
- There is no physical way to logically perform the chosen action. ( spaces that are too tight)

### Avoiding

Avoiding represents an attempt to slip past another character without direct confrontation. You can attempt this as part of any action that allows movement, provided you have enough remaining movement to move past the blocking character.

Procedure:

- Both characters make an Opposed Dexterity Test.
- Success: The moving character avoids and passes the blocker.
- Failure: The blocking character prevents passage.
- Draws favor the blocking character.

### Colliding

Colliding means forcing your way through the character blocking you. You can attempt this part as an Attack Action or a Move Action.

Procedure:

- You must move at least half of your allotted movement ( or your remaining movement, whichever is lower), to a minimum of one Adjustment.
- If there is an obstacle in the way, it becomes the target of your collision.
- Make an Opposed Fitness or Constitution Test.
- Characters can instead Test the Grappling or Striking Skills, and those with a set shield can use the Shield Skill.

Results:

- On a collision, both characters take damage from each other.
- The Base Damage Rating (xDR) equals the opposing character's Size+CON+Speed/2
- When using the Shield Skill, add the Shield's BLK rating to your Collision Damage and its Rating to your defense against the collision.

Considerations:

- Head on collisions add both character's Speed.
- Side collisions only use the Attacker's Speed.
- Pursuing collisions where the target is running away, use the difference between their Speeds.

### Shoving

Shoving represents an attempt to push an opponent out of your path without fully engaging them. You can attempt this as part of any action that allows movement. Provided you have enough movement remaining to move past the blocking character.

Procedure:

- Make an Opposed Fitness or Build Test.
- Success: You move past the blocking character.
- Failure: The blocking character stops you and may choose to initiate a free successful Grapple on their next turn.
- Draws favor the blocking character.

## Climbing

Climbing is a special movement case, as it combines skill with the risk of falling. To move up, down, or across a surface, a character must make a successful Climbing Skill test.

- Success: allows the character to move according to the climbing movement rates table.
- Failure: The character begins loosing their grip.
- Fumble results in the character falling immediately.

Movement rates depend on the condition and complexity of the climb, as well as whether the character climbs with or without urgency.

**Climbing Movement Rates:**

| CPX | Condition of the climb      | Movement with Urgency     | Movement Without Urgency  |
| --- | --------------------------- | ------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| 0   | Rope up                     | $30cm+10*ClimbSkillLvl/s$ | $15cm+5*ClimbSkillLvl/s$  |
| 0   | Rope down                   | $60cm+20*ClimbSkillLvl/s$ | $30cm+10*ClimbSkillLvl/s$ |
| 0   | Ladder                      | $4 rungs/s$               | $2 rungs/s$               |
| 0   | Plenty of handhold/foothold | $30cm+10*ClimbSkillLvl/s$ | $15cm+5*ClimbSkillLvl/s$  |
| 1   | Sparse handhold/foothold    | $15cm+5*ClimbSkillLvl/s$  | $5cm+1*ClimbSkillLvl/s$   |
| 2   | Narrow handhold/foothold    | $10cm+5*ClimbSkillLvl/s$  | $5cm+1*ClimbSkillLvl/s$   |
| 3   | Smooth handhold/foothold    | $10cm+5*ClimbSkillLvl/s$  | $5cm+1*ClimbSkillLvl/s$   |

**Climbing Thresholds:**

| Thresholds | Surface            | Rope                                      |
| ---------- | ------------------ | ----------------------------------------- |
| 2          | 20 degree slope    | Knotted rope with a wall to brace against |
| 3          | 30 degree slope    | Smooth rope with a wall to brace against  |
| 4          | 45 degree slope    | Horizontal rope                           |
| 5          | 70 degree slope    | Vertical knotted rope                     |
| 6          | 90 degree wall     | Vertical smooth rope                      |
| 7          | 100 degree ceiling | N/A                                       |
| 8          | 110 degree ceiling | N/A                                       |
| 9          | 135 degree ceiling | N/A                                       |
| 12         | flat ceiling       | N/A                                       |

### Surface Modifiers

It is assumed that all climbing checks are made on a surface that offers moderate grip, Adjust the Threshold by the following values according to the surface quality.

| Modifier | Quality                                        |
| -------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| -3       | Wet and extremely slippery (e.g., water slide) |
| -2       | Slick with oils                                |
| -1       | Wet or Muddy                                   |
| +1       | Dry with mild traction                         |
| +2       | Rough enough to scrape or cut on a fall        |
| +3       | Highly adhesive, sandpaper, or tar-like        |

### Losing Your Grip

On a Failed Climbing test, the climber begins to lose grip.

- Gains a -1 modifier to subsequent Climbing tests.
- Each additional failure adds a cumulative -1
- When the Threshold can no longer be met, the climber falls.

### Regaining Your Grip

A character can Spend Take Time Actions to Regain grip more easily.

- Spend a number of Take Time Actions equal to your penalty.
- Make a Climbing Test with a Threshold of 0 modified by the current climbing penalties.
- Success: remove your penalties.
- Failure: you continue loosing your grip. Add another -1 to modifiers.

### Hanging

If a climber has no footing it must hang by their arms. After 30 seconds + (30 seconds \* FIT + Climbing CPX) of hanging, make a Fitness Attribute test or begin to loose grip.

### Climbing During Combat

Climbing makes defending difficult:

- -2D penalty for Enabled Defense while climbing.
- Both hands are required to climb.

If the character is wounded while climbing:

- Make a Climbing or Fitness Test.
- Failure means the character falls from their current height.

### Catching Yourself

It is nearly impossible to grab onto something mid fall. However, a character can attempt a Fitness or Climbing Test with a Threshold of 3+'Current Climb Threshold' to do so. it is recommended to rely on safety gear to avoid deadly falls while climbing.

## Digging, Picking, and Shoveling

These actions are resolved as Work Tests modified by the Fitness (FIT) Attribute of the characters involved. The Referee determines the total Work Points (WP) required to complete the task. All soil must be picked before shoveled, except loose soil

Machines or tools that perform these actions contribute a fixed number of Work Points per day according to their descriptions.

Soil Work Costs (cubic meters)

| Type of Soil     | Work Points for picking | Work points for Shoveling |
| ---------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Loose soil, sand | N/A                     | $1WP*1m^3$                |
| Regular soil     | $1WP*2m^3$              | $1WP*1m^3$                |
| Hard soil        | $1WP*1m^3$              | $1WP*1m^3$                |
| Rocky soil       | $1WP*0.5m^3$            | $1WP*0.5m^3$              |

To calculate the total Work Points required, determine the volume of material to be worked, then apply the appropriate multiplier from the table above.

## Hiking

Hiking represents sustained movement over long distances and periods of time. It is resolved as a Work Test using either the Fitness Attribute or the Hiking Skill.

The Referee sets the total Work Points needed to reach the destination based on terrain complexity.

| CPX | Adjectives   | Description                                       | Work Point Cost |
| --- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------- | --------------- |
| 0   | Good Terrain | Hard soil, trails, roads                          | $1WP*4km$       |
| 1   | Average      | Light forest, grassy hills                        | $1WP*2km$       |
| 2   | Bad          | Forest, streams, steep hills, snow                | $1WP*1km$       |
| 3   | Very Bad     | Dense forest/jungle, mountains, soft sand, swamps | $2WP*1km$       |
| 4   | Extreme      | Deep snow, deep swam, rocky mountains             | $3WP*1km$       |

### Weather Effect on Hiking

| Condition | WP Modifier               |
| --------- | ------------------------- |
| Rain      | 2x If it can turn muddy   |
| Snow      | 2x If not using snowshoes |
| Ice       | 2x In any terrain         |

## Falling

Falling is treated as if the ground were attacking the falling character. When a character falls:

- Determine the Relevant Skill (Contortion, Jumping, Riding, Zero-G, Diving)
- Falling dice equal to the meters fallen, up to a maximum of 10 dice.
- The Skill Level removes 1 meter fallen per Lvl, Falling dice still can reach the 10 maximum.
- The Damage Rating (xDR) equals the number of meters fallen, up to a maximum of 30 meters.
- The Skill Complexity removes -1xDR per CPX Level.
- The Damage is multiplied by the Gravity of the planet (1G for Earth).
- The Character takes the damage total.
- If the conditions of the fall cause it to hit an extremity first, the attack also targets the extremity.

### Impact Surfaces

Base falling damage assumes that a moderately hard surface, such as grass or packed soil. Adjust the effective fall distance according to the impact surface.

Falling into water:

- The water surface is the point of impact.
- Reduce the distance of the fall by two meters per meter of water Dived. Up to 10 meters of water.
- Characters do not sink beyond 10 meters deep before water breaks their fall.
- If the water is shallower than 10 meters. With the new fall distance being the distance adjusted considering the water.

### Gear against falling

Gear meant to rescue people falling reduce the distance fallen by their rating.

## Jumping

Jumping distance is determined by the Ground Movement Speed used the turn before the jump. Both vertical and horizontal jumps are calculated as fractions of the distance moved immediately prior to jumping. Machines that are not using the Walker movement must use a ramp.

- Vertical Jump Distance (90°): $\frac{CPX}{12} * \text{Distance Moved}$

- Horizontal Jump Distance (45°): $\frac{CPX}{6} * \text{Distance Moved}$

Jumping distance is divided by the Gravity of the Planet (1G for Earth).

To jump, a character must take a Threshold 4 Personal Action Skill Test. The Skill used are the Jumping Skill for characters and Pilot Skill for Machines. The maximum movement type that contributes to jump distance depends on the Skill Complexity Level. the turn spent jumping is a Take Time Action where the character changes postures crouching and eventually jumping. if circumstances prevent them from performing these 2 movements, halve the distance.

| Gear   | Distance Jumped | CPX |
| ------ | --------------- | --- |
| 2nd    | Walking         | 0   |
| 3rd    | Jogging         | 1   |
| 4th    | Running         | 2   |
| 5th    | Sprinting       | 3   |
| 2\*5th | Sprinting x 2   | 4   |
| 3\*5th | Sprinting x 3   | 5   |

## Swimming

The Swimming Skill is used whenever a character must move through water deep enough their feet cannot touch the ground.

A character's base swimming speed in calm water is:

- Base Swim Speed = 100cm + (10cm\*FIT)

Professional or trained swimmers gain an additional 20cm per Complexity Level of their Swimming Skill.

| Threshold | Adjective                    | Description                                                                        |
| --------- | ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0         | Special                      | Technically not a test but used as part of a special rule.                         |
| 1         | Effortless                   | Floating with a lifesaver                                                          |
| 2         | Routine                      | Floating with a floating toy                                                       |
| 3         | Easy                         | Floating with no aid                                                               |
| 4         | Moderate                     | Swimming in a Regular Pool/ Dead Water                                             |
| 5         | Challenging                  | Beach with non-breaking waves, calm rivers                                         |
| 6         | Difficult                    | Beach with breaking waves, open rivers                                             |
| 7         | Very Difficult               | Rivers with several changes in currents and obstacles                              |
| 8         | Extremely impossible         | Rivers with several changes in currents, obstacles, small falls                    |
| 10        | Near impossible              | Rivers with several changes in currents, obstacles, small falls after a large rain |
| 12+       | Pray for divine intervention | Drowning machine phenomena                                                         |

### Going Underwater

On a Failed Swimming Test, the swimmer begins sinking.

While underwater:

- The swimmer cannot breathe.
- Suffers a -1 penalty to further Swimming tests.
- Each additional failure adds another -1 cumulative penalty.
- If the swimmer cannot meet the threshold with rolls, drowning is imminent.

### Going Above Water

A swimmer can use Take Time Actions attempting to surface.

- Make a Swimming test with a threshold of 0.
- Success: You remove the penalties and surface.
- On a Failure: You remain submerged and keep adding -1 to the penalty.

### Floating

In calm water, a character can remain afloat without active swimming. While floating, they drift with currents but keep their head above water. Strong waves or turbulence can force Periodic Swimming tests to remain stable.

### Swimming During Combat

Combat while swimming is difficult and dangerous:

- -2D penalty for Enabled Defense while swimming.
- Both legs must be free to swim effectively.
- Any time a swimmer is wounded, they must make an immediate Swimming Skill test.
- If they fail they start to sink.

Certain gear can grant bonuses to swimming speed or automatic floating at the Referee's discretion.

### Saving another Swimmer

Attempting to save a drowning person is a CPX 3 Swimming Test.

- Rescuers must roll with the same penalties that apply to the drowning individual.
- Success: The drowning swimmer is stabilized and brought to the surface.
- Failure: The drowning swimmer keeps gaining penalties.
- Fumble: both characters start sinking.

## Holding Your Breath

Characters often need to hold their breath while swimming underwater or during environmental hazards. The capacity of held breath depends on the intensity of the activity being performed.

| Intensity of Activity | Adjective                 | Time          |
| --------------------- | ------------------------- | ------------- |
| No activity           | Sitting, meditating       | $90s+30s*FIT$ |
| Mild activity         | Walking, driving          | $30s+10s*FIT$ |
| Heavy Activity        | Combat, running, climbing | $15s+5s*FIT$  |

- If the character has time to prepare, add half the calculated duration to the total.

### Drowning

When the time limit expires. Your character gains 1 fatigue per second of drowning.

## Lifting, Dragging, and Carrying Capacity

A character's physical carrying ability is based on their Basic Lift, derived from their Fitness Attribute Modifier FIT using the Constitution Attribute Table CON. Use the FIT modifier and find the equivalent weight as shown on the CON table. For example, a character with a +2 CON has a basic lift of 90kg. Use the following table to determine specific lifting and movement capabilities:

| Movement           | Capacity         | Description                                                           |
| ------------------ | ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Dead-lift          | $Basic Lift$     | Amount you can lift off the ground and hold without moving            |
| Grab               | $0.4*Basic Lift$ | Amount you can lift off the ground move about                         |
| Push (bench press) | $0.4*Basic Lift$ | Amount you can push over your chest when laying down                  |
| Overhead Press     | $0.3*Basic Lift$ | Amount you can lift over your head                                    |
| One-handed curl    | $0.1*Basic Lift$ | Amount you can pick with a single hand and raise up to your shoulders |
| Two-handed curl    | $0.2*Basic Lift$ | Amount you can pick with a single hand and raise up to your shoulders |

### Encumbrance

Encumbrance represents the total load distributed across a character's body.

| Carrying Capacity           | Encumbrance Effect             |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| $0$ to $0.5*Basic Lift$     | No Penalty                     |
| $0.51$ to $0.75*Basic Lift$ | Can't move faster than jogging |
| $0.76$ to $Basic Lift$      | Can't move faster than walk    |
| Above $Basic Lift$          | Can't Walk                     |

### Bulk Rating Encumbrance

Weight tracking can become cumbersome in play. The Bulk Rating Encumbrance abstracts carrying capacity into a simplified inventory limit.

A characters BLK Capacity is:

- BLK Capacity = 15+2*CON+2*CRE

Each carried item has a BLK rating, which is subtracted from your total capacity. Items with BLK 0 are negligible, however many fit into a palm count for a BLK 1 equivalent.

Containers such as bags, quivers, or backpacks have a BLK Capacity equal to their Rating, allowing them to hold more items than their personal BLK cost.

### Pulling and Dragging

When pulling or dragging an object, apply the same rules as carrying but modify the load's effective weight according to how it is being moved:

| Dragging situation | Modifier                                   |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------ |
| Unassisted         | Use the full weight                        |
| A sleigh           | Add the sleigh weight then divide all by 4 |
| Two wheeled carts  | Add the cart weight then divide all by 10  |
| Four wheeled cart  | Add the cart weight then divide all by 20  |

These modifiers assume smooth, solid ground. Difficult terrain may double the effective weight or make dragging impossible, like when wheels sink into mud.

### Moving Objects During Combat

During Combat:

- Picking objects up to your curling weight requires the Take Time Action.
- Heavier objects require two Take Time Actions. One to get in position and one for the movement.
- To lift an unwilling character, they must first be pinned using the Grappling rules. They can attempt to break free afterward.
- You can shove objects weighing up to your Basic Lift if you have sufficient space to move.

## Throwing & Catching

Throwing launches objects towards a target, characters throwing by hand use the Throwing Skill, weapons with the Launcher SpecialFx use the respective weapon Skill.

### Targeting Throws

Static points:

- Make a Ranged Attack using the throwing Skill (modified normally).
- The target has an Disabled Defense '1'.
- If your total meets or exceeds the defense, the object lands on target.

Moving Opponent:

- Make a Ranged Attack using the Throwing Skill (modified normally)
- The opponent, evades, blocks, or deflects the Attack.

Misses:

- The projectile deviates 1 meter per DoF.
- Roll 1d6 for a random direction. 1 is straight ahead from the thrower, continue the rest of the results clockwise.

Fumbles: The object deviates towards the thrower. Double the Miss distance according to the DoF.

### Hand Thrown Distances

This assumes a popper projectile (e.g., a stone, not a feather).

Base throwing range:

- Weight: minimum 1kg (for calculation purposes).
- Base Range = 0.1 \* Basic Lift/Weight (in meters).

Damage Multiplier:

- $xDR= TBD+(Weight)+Throwing CPX.$

### Catching Objects

When an object is launched to a wiling recipient:

- The Thrower makes a Skill Test as if the target was a static point.
- The Degree of Success becomes a modifier for the Catcher.
- The Catcher makes a Threshold 4 Agility Attribute Test to successfully catch it.
